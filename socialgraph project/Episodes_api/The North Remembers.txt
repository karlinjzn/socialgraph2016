 <p></p> <p><b>"The North Remembers"</b> is the first episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the eleventh episode of the series overall. It premiered on April 1, 2012. It was written by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> and directed by co-executive producer <a href="/wiki/Alan_Taylor" title="Alan Taylor">Alan Taylor</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p>Under the burning <a href="/wiki/Red_Comet" title="Red Comet">Red Comet</a> in the sky, war grips the continent of <a href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. In <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> arrives at court to serve as acting <a href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>, and to rein in his crazed nephew King <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a>, as well as Queen Regent <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a>. At Dragonstone, <a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a> begins his quest to take the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a> with the help of a <a href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">mysterious foreign priestess</a>. On the eastern continent, <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a> leads her people through the <a href="/wiki/Red_Waste" title="Red Waste">Red Waste</a>. Beyond <a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ranger" title="Ranger">rangers</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Lord Commander</a> <a href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a>, seek help from a dangerous <a href="/wiki/Craster" title="Craster">wildling</a>. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="Beyond_the_Wall" section="3">Beyond the Wall</h3> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":4}' data-ref="4"><a href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/61/Sam%2C_Edd_and_Jon_2x01.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160813043828"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20848%20474'%2F%3E" alt="Sam, Edd and Jon 2x01.jpg" width="848" height="474"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/61/Sam%2C_Edd_and_Jon_2x01.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160813043828" alt="Sam, Edd and Jon 2x01.jpg" width="848" height="474"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>The Night's Watch approaches Crasters Keep.</figcaption></figure><p><a href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Lord Commander</a> <a href="/wiki/Jeor_Mormont" title="Jeor Mormont">Jeor Mormont</a>'s rangers arrive at <a href="/wiki/Craster%27s_Keep" title="Craster's Keep">Craster's Keep</a> having passed several abandoned <a href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildling</a> villages. <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grenn" title="Grenn">Grenn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dolorous_Edd" title="Dolorous Edd" class="mw-redirect">Dolorous Edd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> are repulsed by <a href="/wiki/Craster" title="Craster">Craster</a>, who has many wives, most of whom are also his own daughters, but who seemingly has no sons. Craster tells Jeor that he has not seen the missing <a href="/wiki/Benjen_Stark" title="Benjen Stark">Benjen Stark</a> and reports that the other wildlings are assembling for the <a href="/wiki/King-Beyond-the-Wall" title="King-Beyond-the-Wall">King-Beyond-the-Wall</a> <a href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a>. Jon challenges their host, who is low and crude, but is demanding and demeaning when he is talking to the noble Commander Mormont (who ignores his insulting behavior). When Craster refers to them as southerners, Jon argues with him. Jeor reprimands Jon, noting that Craster may be disgusting, but the Night's Watch needs him. His information and shelter in the harsh conditions north of the Wall have helped them many times before. He is irritated by Ser <a href="/wiki/Dontos_Hollard" title="Dontos Hollard">Dontos Hollard</a>, who arrives both late and intoxicated for his bout. Joffrey orders his guards to pour an entire barrel of wine down Dontos' throat with a funnel. <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> intercedes telling Joffrey that ordering a death on one's name day is bad luck. Joffrey dismisses this as a mindless superstition, but <a href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a> seconds her. Cersei orders the city gates closed to refugees from the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">war in the Riverlands</a>. When Tyrion arrives with a letter from Lord Tywin, appointing him as acting <a href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a> in Tywin's stead, Cersei becomes livid. She is suspicious that Tyrion is taking power for himself, but he convinces her that he is there to act as an advisor. He criticizes his sister for failing to rein in Joffrey and overseeing numerous bad decisions, particularly the <a href="/wiki/Execution_of_Eddard_Stark" title="Execution of Eddard Stark">execution</a> of <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Ned Stark</a>. Their actions have sparked a war, which, he adds, their side is currently losing. He is even more incredulous when he learns that <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> escaped, pointing out how much stronger their position could have been with three Stark <a href="/wiki/Hostage" title="Hostage">hostages</a>. Tyrion hides his lover <a href="/wiki/Shae" title="Shae">Shae</a> in his chambers in the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_the_Hand" title="Tower of the Hand">Tower of the Hand</a> because he was forbidden by his father to bring her with him. She is initially thrilled at being brought to the capital, and says that big cities make her "want to fuck". </p> <p>At <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a> suffers through the petty complaints of his vassals, facing unrest at <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> calling his banners and taking the young men south. <a href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">Maester</a> <a href="/wiki/Luwin" title="Luwin">Luwin</a> guides him in carrying out a lord's duties. He has <a href="/wiki/Osha" title="Osha">Osha</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hodor" title="Hodor">Hodor</a> take him to the pool and is reassured to find that his waking reflection is his own. They discuss the phenomenon of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Comet" title="Red Comet">Red Comet</a>, as many people think it means different things. Melisandre proclaims Stannis a champion of the Lord of Light, as he draws a <a href="/wiki/Lightbringer" title="Lightbringer">burning sword</a> from one of the statues. She responds by threatening his life in a demonstration of her control over her guards, ordering them to first seize Baelish and cut his throat and almost immediately rescinding the order. It is chiefly used in the context of a warning, in the same way as "Winter is coming" or "A Lannister always pays his debts", that the people of the North will get even with those who wrong them, especially the Lannisters and their allies. </li> <li>As the HBO behind the scenes featurettes point out, it took days if not weeks for Tyrion to travel to King's Landing from the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Green_Fork" title="Battle of the Green Fork">Battle of the Green Fork</a>, so logically he wouldn't be wearing heavy armor that entire time. There was a considerable amount of discussion about what Tyrion should be wearing. Ultimately it was decided that Tyrion intentionally changed back into his armor just before he arrived, and this is a subtle hint about how he's choosing to present himself, both to Joffrey and to Cersei. Tyrion wants to rub it in their faces that he has been doing more to actively help the war effort than either of them, a war they started by Joffrey foolishly executing Ned Stark, while Joffrey and Cersei spent the whole time in King's Landing essentially just waiting for Lord Tywin to clean up the mess. They're even holding a tournament in honor of Joffrey's nameday while <i>over half</i> of the realm has risen in rebellion. This makes Tyrion's barb to Joffrey that "We looked for you on the battlefield, and you were nowhere to be found" all the more pointed. </li> <li> <a href="/wiki/Carice_van_Houten" title="Carice van Houten">Carice van Houten</a> was previously asked to audition for a different role when season 1 was shot, but schedule conflicts prevented her from auditioning. She was asked to come back to audition during shooting of season 2, and was cast as <a href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">Melisandre</a>. </li> <li>In the books, Daenerys' <a href="/wiki/Silver" title="Silver" class="mw-redirect">silver mare</a> does survive the journey through Red Waste on a diet of devilgrass. While edible, the grass was colored brown and grew in dry areas. </li> <li> <a href="/wiki/Josephine_Gillan" title="Josephine Gillan">Josephine Gillan</a> was only credited as new prostitute and only in Season 3 was her character's name was revealed as <a href="/wiki/Marei" title="Marei">Marei</a>. </li> <li>Two of <a href="/wiki/Craster%27s_wives" title="Craster's wives">Craster's wives</a> that appear in the background are actually director <a href="/wiki/Alan_Taylor" title="Alan Taylor">Alan Taylor</a>'s daughters, dressed up as wildlings as a cameo.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[4]</a></sup> </li> <li>Robb has no way to know that Jaime pushed Bran from the window. Tyrion finally arrives in capital and tells Joffrey that there is work to be done. </li> <li> Chapter 3, Tyrion I: Tyrion meets with the small council and assumes his position as acting Hand of the King.
