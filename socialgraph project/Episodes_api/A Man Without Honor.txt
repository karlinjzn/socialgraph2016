 <p></p> <p><b>"A Man Without Honor"</b> is the seventh episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the seventeenth episode of the series overall. It premiered on May 13, 2012. It was written by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/David_Nutter" title="David Nutter">David Nutter</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p><a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime</a> meets a relative; <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon</a> hunts; <a href="/wiki/Daenerys" title="Daenerys" class="mw-redirect">Dany</a> receives an invitation. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="In_the_Seven_Kingdoms" section="3">In the Seven Kingdoms</h3> <p>At <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>, now held by the <a href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a>, <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> awakens to find that <a href="/wiki/Osha" title="Osha">Osha</a> is gone and she has escaped with <a href="/wiki/Hodor" title="Hodor">Hodor</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">Rickon Stark</a> and their <a href="/wiki/Direwolves" title="Direwolves">direwolves</a>. Theon berates <a href="/wiki/Lorren" title="Lorren">Lorren</a> for allowing them to escape. When Lorren counters that Theon is also to blame for sleeping with Osha and letting his guard down, Theon furiously beats him. Theon leads the ironborn on horse to hunt down the boys with hounds to track them. The search leads them to a shepherd's farm, but the farmer says that he hasn't seen anyone. Dagmer finds some walnut shells, so they know that Bran and Rickon were there, but the hounds can't get the scent. Theon tells them the hunt is over and to return <a href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">Maester</a> <a href="/wiki/Luwin" title="Luwin">Luwin</a> to Winterfell: he knows where they are, and his mercy has been exhausted. </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":3}' data-ref="3"><a href="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/af/Burned_bodies.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160719232539"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20768%20432'%2F%3E" alt="Burned bodies.jpg" width="768" height="432"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/af/Burned_bodies.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160719232539" alt="Burned bodies.jpg" width="768" height="432"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Theon has the burned bodies raised on the walls of Winterfell</figcaption></figure><p>Theon returns to Winterfell with two children's bodies burned beyond recognition, and he has them hoisted to either side of the main gate. Enraged, Tywin orders Gregor to burn out villages and farms in reprisal for this assassination attempt. Tywin later suspects that Arya is highborn, because she doesn't pronounce "M'Lord" the way commoners do, instead saying "My Lord," and possesses an educated vocabulary. Arya makes the excuse that her Mother, whom she said was a handmaiden for years to royalty, had taught her to speak properly like them. In <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> comes to thank <a href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor "The Hound" Clegane</a> for saving her life during <a href="/wiki/Riot_of_King%27s_Landing" title="Riot of King's Landing">the recent riot</a> in the city and says that he was very brave. Sandor simply says that a dog doesn't need courage to fight off rats. Cersei presents Sansa with warped advice on Joffrey, bizarre counseling based on her bitter marriage with King <a href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>. Blithely waving away her son's torment of Sansa and homicidal outbursts she says that her brother <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a> stayed by her side the whole time she was giving birth to Joffrey, but Sansa should never expect such devotion from Joffrey. Cersei says that no one expects her to love Joffrey, though she may take comfort in her love for the children they will have. </p> <p>Privately, Cersei meets with Tyrion and expresses regret at having children with her own brother Jaime. Ygritte says that Jon should have taken her when he had the chance. </p> <h3 id="In_Qarth" section="5">In Qarth</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> is desperate to find her stolen <a href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragons</a>. id="Notes" section="12">Notes</h2> <ul> <li>The title of the episode comes from a line by <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> to <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a>, the Kingslayer: "You are a man without honor." </li> <li>Tywin Lannister states that the current civil war in the Seven Kingdoms is being called "the War of Five Kings." This is the first time that the conflict has been named on-screen - though the formal name is ultimately the "<a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>." It is referred to in dialogue as the "War of <i>the</i> Five Kings" <a href="/wiki/The_Lion_and_the_Rose" title="The Lion and the Rose">in Season 4</a>. </li> <li>In the "ThronesCast" interview series, actor Nikolaj Coster-Waldau (who plays Jaime Lannister) mysteriously refers to a scene in this episode as his favorite moment in the series and his favorite scene he's ever acted in. He later explained in the HBO featurette for this episode that he was referring to the scene between Jaime and Alton Lannister.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup> </li> <li>Tyrion made comments similar to Ygritte's back in Season 1 when he visited the Wall, pointing out that the "<a href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildlings</a>" are basically just those people whose ancestors were unfortunate enough to happen to be living north of the Wall when it was built. Both the inhabitants of <a href="/wiki/The_North" title="The North">the North</a> (south of the Wall, ruled by the Starks) and the wildlings are descended from the <a href="/wiki/First_Men" title="First Men">First Men</a>. They have much closer ethnic ties to each other than the foreign <a href="/wiki/Andal" title="Andal" class="mw-redirect">Andal</a> invaders who now dominate all of southern Westeros. Both groups even have the same religion, worshiping the <a href="/wiki/Old_Gods_of_the_Forest" title="Old Gods of the Forest">Old Gods of the Forest</a>, as <a href="/wiki/Osha" title="Osha">Osha</a> pointed out in Season 1. </li> <li>According to episode director David Nutter, the episode was filmed not long after the footage of Libyan dictator Muammar Gaddafi's death was circulating heavily in the news, during which Gaddafi was surrounded by a frenzied mob eager to exact "eye for an eye" vengeance. Nutter said that he was loosely inspired by the visual energy of this footage when he was shooting the scene in which Jaime Lannister has been recaptured after killing Rickard Karstark's son, and is dragged back into camp by an angry mob of Northern soldiers who want to immediately kill him.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup> </li> </ul> <h2 id="In_the_books" section="13">In the books</h2> <dl><dd> <i>See: <a href="/wiki/Differences_between_books_and_TV_series_-_Season_2#A_Man_Without_Honor" title="Differences between books and TV series - Season 2">Differences between books and TV series - Season 2#A Man Without Honor</a> </i> </dd></dl> <ul><li> The episode is adapted from the following chapters of <i>A Clash of Kings</i>: <ul> <li> Chapter 20, Tyrion IV: Cersei breaks down about the safety of her children. </li> <li> Chapter 26, Arya VI: Arya recalls the burning of Harrenhal. </li> <li> Chapter 30, Arya VII: Arya recalls the burning of Harrenhal. </li> <li> Chapter 39, Catelyn V: Jaime makes an escape attempt. </li> <li> Chapter 40, Daenerys III: Daenerys considers the invitation to the House of the Undying. </li> <li> Chapter 45, Catelyn VI: Catelyn questions the returning envoy about peace terms. </li> <li> Chapter 50, Theon IV: Theon sleeps with a Winterfell servant. The escape of Bran and Rickon is reported to him and he mounts a hunt. He eventually loses the scent and decides to kill them if he finds them. A subordinate suggests that they are hiding nearby. </li> <li> Chapter 51, Jon VI: Jon speaks with Ygritte as his captive. </li> <li> Chapter 52, Sansa IV: Sansa thanks Sandor for saving her during the riots.
