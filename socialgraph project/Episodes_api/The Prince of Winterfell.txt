Ygritte convinces him to spare Jon because of his Stark blood. Qhorin initiates a plan to install Jon as a spy within the <a href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildling</a> ranks. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Grenn" title="Grenn">Grenn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> discover a cache of <a href="/wiki/Dragonglass" title="Dragonglass">dragonglass</a> weapons at the <a href="/wiki/Fist_of_the_First_Men" title="Fist of the First Men">Fist of the First Men</a>. Luwin urges her not to tell <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">Rickon</a> about the children Theon had murdered, but Bran is sitting up behind a corner and listening to them. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>'s fleet is closing in on <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. He takes advice from <a href="/wiki/Bronn" title="Bronn">Bronn</a> and <a href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> and infers that he has decided to make use of the stocks of <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a>. He decides to march west, leaving <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> at <a href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a> to serve his castellan, Ser <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a>. She forces him to help her by giving him his own name as the third death he promised. She will only revoke his name when he agrees to break her and her friends out of the fortress. Arya, <a href="/wiki/Hot_Pie" title="Hot Pie">Hot Pie</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Gendry" title="Gendry">Gendry</a> walk through the gates at midnight, as Jaqen instructed. They escape Harrenhal, realizing that the guards have been killed. </p> <p>Robb is furious when he learns that his mother <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a> has freed his prized captive Ser <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a>. He tells her that she has betrayed him and orders her kept under guard. Catelyn has sent <a href="/wiki/Brienne_of_Tarth" title="Brienne of Tarth">Brienne of Tarth</a> to escort Jaime to King's Landing to exchange him for her daughters, <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a>. Brienne uses a canoe to evade pursuit. Robb plans to relieve Winterfell by turning Theon's men against him with an offer of mercy, issuing his orders to the <a href="/wiki/Ramsay_Bolton" title="Ramsay Bolton">bastard of the Dreadfort</a> through <a href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a>. Robb seeks solace in the arms of Lady <a href="/wiki/Talisa_Stark" title="Talisa Stark">Talisa Maegyr</a>, ignoring his betrothal to <a href="/wiki/House_Frey" title="House Frey">House Frey</a>. </p> title="Yara Greyjoy">Yara Greyjoy</a> refers to her brother <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> as the <a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Winterfell" title="Prince of Winterfell">Prince of Winterfell</a> in dialogue. Theon styled himself Prince after taking Winterfell. In <i>A Clash of Kings,</i> Theon openly styles himself Prince of Winterfell. </li> <li>Lord Karstark says a line about "the Father," one of the aspects of the godhead in the <a href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a>, but the Karstarks worship the <a href="/wiki/Old_Gods_of_the_Forest" title="Old Gods of the Forest">Old Gods of the Forest</a>. Writer <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Cogman" title="Bryan Cogman">Bryan Cogman</a> responded about this: "the line about Karstark praying to the Father in 208... yeah, I should've caught that. Karstark would be strictly Old Gods, I think. If it helps, you could read it as "I'd even break my faith and pray to the bloody FATHER if that's what it took to bring my sons back"... but I'm not sure that was the intent. But maybe it was. I try my best!"<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup> </li> <li>Tyrion and, by extension, Varys, become the first ongoing characters west of the Narrow Sea to be aware of the survival of <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> and the existence of her dragons. </li> <li>Talisa's speech to Robb Stark explaining her backstory in Volantis is an invention of the TV series. In the books, Talisa's equivalent character is Jeyne Westerling, a noblewoman from a minor Lannister vassal family, House Westerling. This is the only scene in which Talisa ever explains her altered backstory, but the changes do not appear to be fully researched and contain several contradictions: <ul> <li>Robb Stark is around 18 years old in Season 1, because he was conceived at the start of Robert's Rebellion, which ended 17 years ago (increasing his age in the books by two years). Numerous other times in the TV series itself, characters have mentioned that Westeros is at the end of a very long summer which has lasted for 10 years (the start of autumn was officially announced in the Season 2 premiere). When Talisa describes her backstory, she says that she and her brother went to bathe in the Rhoyne River when she was 12 years old, because it was the hottest day in the "three year summer." The 10 year long summer that ended in the Season 2 premiere began when Robb was around 9 years old. It is possible (though unlikely) that an autumn/winter/spring cycle quickly occurred during a single year, preceded by a three-year long summer (the seasons are not regular). In which case this incident could have occurred no more than 11 years ago when Robb was around 5 to 6 years old. Talisa, however, states that she was 12 at the time, meaning she would have to be <i>at least</i> five to six years older than Robb. In the books, Jeyne Westerling is roughly the same age as Robb Stark, and while Talisa in the TV continuity is a separate character, the actors playing Robb and Talisa were actually born within two weeks of each other. When the writers developed this altered backstory for "Talisa" including an off-hand mention of a "three year summer," they apparently didn't consider that the <i>current</i> summer lasted for ten full years. </li> <li>The nobles and slaves of Volantis never mingle, and they are one of the most segregated of the Free Cities. On the other hand, Talisa's story somewhat implies that she and her brother sneaked off because their parents were away at a wedding and they weren't supposed to be in mixed company at the river, and this was just an exceptional incident in which all classes mingled at the river due to a heatwave. </li> <li>Talisa says that drummers were playing for "coppers" on the east bank of the river. "Copper Pennies" are a <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">Currency</a> of the Seven Kingdoms, not Volantis. The Seven Kingdoms use the Gold Dragon coin, of which Silver Stags and Copper Pennies are smaller denominations. Volantis uses round gold coins called "honors"–there may be smaller Volantene denominations made of copper, but they have not been mentioned in the books. </li> <li>Talisa's speech does, however, accurately relate a detail from the books, that slaves in Volantis are given facial tattoos so people can recognize them just by looking at them. </li> </ul> </li> </ul> <h2 id="Memorable_quotes" section="13">Memorable quotes</h2> <p><b><a href="/wiki/Yara_Greyjoy" title="Yara Greyjoy">Yara Greyjoy</a></b>: "Which one gave <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">you</a> the tougher fight, the <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">cripple</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">six-year-old</a>?" </p> <p><b><a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a></b>: "Do you know why Varys is so dangerous?"<br><b><a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a></b>: "Because he has thousands of spies in his employ?
