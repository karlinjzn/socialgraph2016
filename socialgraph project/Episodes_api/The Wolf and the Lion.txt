 <p></p> <p><b>"The Wolf and the Lion"</b> is the fifth episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones_(TV_series)" title="Game of Thrones (TV series)">Game of Thrones</a></i> and the fifth episode of the series overall. It first aired on May 15, 2011. It was written by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Kirk" title="Brian Kirk">Brian Kirk</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p><a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a>'s predicament becomes common knowledge in <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>, making life difficult for <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>. Ned's position is already precarious due to a disagreement with King <a href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> over plans to assassinate <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a> before she can rally the <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> to invade <a href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>. In <a href="/wiki/The_Eyrie" title="The Eyrie">the Eyrie</a>, <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn</a> is shocked by her sister <a href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa</a>'s paranoia and distrust. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="In_the_Vale" section="3"> In the Vale </h3> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":2}' data-ref="2"><a href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/b/b0/In_the_hills.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190617"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="In the hills.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/b/b0/In_the_hills.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190617" alt="In the hills.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption><a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> and his escorts in the hills of <a href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale</a>.</figcaption></figure><p>Tyrion Lannister has his blindfold removed and finds that their party, augmented by several knights and the <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellsword</a> <a href="/wiki/Bronn" title="Bronn">Bronn</a> from the <a href="/wiki/Crossroads_Inn" title="Crossroads Inn" class="mw-redirect">Crossroads Inn</a>, is on the <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Road" title="Eastern Road">Eastern Road</a> en route to <a href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale of Arryn</a>. Catelyn had been saying that they were riding for <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a> often and very loudly. Tyrion congratulates her on her cunning, while pointing out that his father will give out a handsome reward for his safe return, a point that clearly piques Bronn's interest. Tyrion says that Lady Lysa Arryn has gone mad since her husband's death and will try to kill him in the Eyrie. He demand his innocence be known in the attempt on <a href="/wiki/Bran" title="Bran" class="mw-redirect">Bran</a>'s life and points out he'd have to be an idiot to give the assassin his own blade. Before Catelyn can answer, the party comes under attack from one of the <a href="/wiki/Hill_tribes" title="Hill tribes">hill tribes</a>. Despite taking several losses, the party is able to drive off the attackers. Though having the opportunity to escape, Tyrion fights, killing his first man in the skirmish, and in doing so, saves Catelyn's life. Afterwards, they resume their trek to the Eyrie. </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":3}' data-ref="3"><a href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/85/Tyrion%2C_Cat_and_Vardis_at_the_Eyrie.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20101128162426"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20360%20480'%2F%3E" alt="Tyrion, Cat and Vardis at the Eyrie.jpg" width="360" height="480"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/85/Tyrion%2C_Cat_and_Vardis_at_the_Eyrie.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20101128162426" alt="Tyrion, Cat and Vardis at the Eyrie.jpg" width="360" height="480"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Tyrion and Catelyn in the Eyrie's High Hall.</figcaption></figure><p>In the High Hall of the Eyrie, Lady Lysa Arryn holds court on behalf of her young son, <a href="/wiki/Robin_Arryn" title="Robin Arryn">Robin</a>. Lysa is furious that Cat has endangered them all by bringing Tyrion to the Vale. Tyrion points out that if he is harmed, there will be war with <a href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a>, but Lysa is unconcerned about his threats, as the Vale is strongly-defended and the Eyrie is impregnable. She orders him sent to the sky cells: three-sided cells with the fourth open to a sheer drop down the mountainside. This forces Tyrion to huddle in a corner and try not to roll out to his death in his sleep. </p> <h3 id="In_the_North" section="4"> In the North </h3> <p>In Winterfell, Bran is angry about his mother's absence. <a href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">Maester</a> <a href="/wiki/Luwin" title="Luwin">Luwin</a> tries to set his mind at rest about the matter, to no avail. However, when Bran gets frustrated at not being able to practice his archery, Luwin suggests that he could learn to use a shortbow, of the kind used in mounted combat by the <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a>, a notion which pleases Bran.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":4}' data-ref="4"><a href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/85/Bran_and_Luwin.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190652"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Bran and Luwin.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/85/Bran_and_Luwin.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190652" alt="Bran and Luwin.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption><a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran</a> and <a href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">Maester</a> <a href="/wiki/Luwin" title="Luwin">Luwin</a> discuss geography.</figcaption></figure><p>Elsewhere in the castle, <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> enjoys the attentions of his favorite <a href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">whore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ros" title="Ros">Ros</a>, but becomes annoyed when she compares his prowess unfavorably to that of Tyrion Lannister. </p> <h3 id="In_King.27s_Landing" section="5"> In King's Landing </h3> <p>Lord Eddard Stark arrives at the tourney ground to speak to Lord Commander <a href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> about the death of Ser <a href="/wiki/Hugh" title="Hugh">Hugh</a> of the Vale. Barristan notes that he had no family or friends so he stood the <a href="/wiki/Knighthood" title="Knighthood">knight</a>'s vigil himself for Ser Hugh. Eddard is suspicious of Ser Hugh's death, wanting to know how he could afford a new set of armor so soon after being made a knight. Barristan reflects on the irony of them being on opposite sides of the war during <a href="/wiki/Robert%27s_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> and now they are working together.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":5}' data-ref="5"><a href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/80/Barristan_and_Eddard.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190801"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Barristan and Eddard.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/80/Barristan_and_Eddard.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190801" alt="Barristan and Eddard.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Ser <a href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> and Lord <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> discuss the death of Ser <a href="/wiki/Hugh" title="Hugh">Hugh</a>.</figcaption></figure><p>He also tells Eddard that King Robert is planning to joust during the day's activities. Ned finds King Robert tormenting his squire, <a href="/wiki/Lancel_Lannister" title="Lancel Lannister">Lancel Lannister</a>, finally sending him off on a snipe hunt in amused disgust. Robert is adamant about fighting in the joust but Ned tells him that he is too fat for his armor and the other knights would let him win. This frustrates Robert but he is forced to admit that Ned is right but at least they can watch the joust from the stands. </p> <p>The first tilt of the day is between the fearsome Ser <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a>, the Mountain, and the popular 'Knight of the Flowers', Ser <a href="/wiki/Loras_Tyrell" title="Loras Tyrell">Loras Tyrell</a>. Loras gives a beautiful flower to <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a>, while <a href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Littlefinger</a> and Lord <a href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a> make a wager on who will win with Renly betting on Loras. Loras is riding a mare that is in heat, which agitates and excites the Mountain's stallion, causing him to throw his rider.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":6}' data-ref="6"><a href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/65/Robert_and_Lancel.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190823"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Robert and Lancel.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/65/Robert_and_Lancel.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190823" alt="Robert and Lancel.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>King <a href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a> is dismayed to find he no longer fits his armor.</figcaption></figure><p>Enraged at having lost, Gregor first decapitates his horse and then attacks Ser Loras. <a href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a> comes to Loras's defense, preventing his brother from killing him. They exchange several blows, until King Robert orders them to cease. Sandor immediately bows to the king, while Gregor storms off the field. Ser Loras declares Sandor the victor of the match to the Hound's discomfort. </p> <p>In the <a href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a>, Lord <a href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> meets with Eddard. Varys tells him that the king is a fool and will soon be dead unless Ned can save him. Ned wants to know why Varys is telling him this now. Varys tells him he had to be sure Ned could be trusted .He calls Ned an honorable and true man, maybe the only one in the city. He also confirms that <a href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a> was assassinated with a poison called the <a href="/wiki/Tears_of_Lys" title="Tears of Lys">Tears of Lys</a> because he "started asking questions".  </p> Later, <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a> is chasing cats in accordance with her sword training. She chases one into the dungeons under the castle, where she finds the great <a href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragon</a> skulls of the <a href="/wiki/Targaryens" title="Targaryens" class="mw-redirect">Targaryens</a> in storage. She hears two men talking: Varys and Magister <a href="/wiki/Illyrio_Mopatis" title="Illyrio Mopatis">Illyrio Mopatis</a> of <a href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a>. Varys says that events have been set in motion and the <a href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">lion</a> and the <a href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">wolf</a> will soon be at each other's throats. War is almost upon the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>, as they planned, but it has come too soon. <a href="/wiki/Khal" title="Khal">Khal</a> <a href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a> will not invade Westeros until his son is born and pronounced healthy, which is still many months away. Varys reveals that he knows about Littlefinger's assistance in Eddard's investigation of Jon Arryn's death and reminds Littlefinger of the consequences that may befall him should the Lannisters discover his involvement. In response, Littlefinger reveals that he already knows about Varys having met with both Eddard and Illyrio just earlier that day, implying he knows of Varys's allegiances across the <a href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>. Their conversation is interrupted by Renly, who informs them that Robert will be joining them at their <a href="/wiki/Small_council" title="Small council" class="mw-redirect">small council</a> meeting. </p> <p>Arya escapes from the dungeon and tries to warn her father about what she overheard but she gets the words muddled up. They are interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Yoren" title="Yoren">Yoren</a>, who has ridden non-stop from the <a href="/wiki/Crossroads_Inn" title="Crossroads Inn" class="mw-redirect">Crossroads Inn</a> to bring Eddard forewarning of Catelyn's arrest of Tyrion Lannister. Ned keeps the information to himself, while Arya asks <a href="/wiki/Jory_Cassel" title="Jory Cassel">Jory Cassel</a> about the state of her father's household guard. Jory tells her she does not have to worry about her father's safety. </p> <p>At the meeting of the small council, King Robert tells Eddard that <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> is pregnant and her son will have a claim to the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Throne" title="Iron Throne">Iron Throne</a>. He wants Daenerys, the unborn child, and <a href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys</a> dead as soon as possible.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":8}' data-ref="8"><a href="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/60/Varys_and_Illyrio.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160805005542"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201680%20945'%2F%3E" alt="Varys and Illyrio.jpg" width="1680" height="945"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/60/Varys_and_Illyrio.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160805005542" alt="Varys and Illyrio.jpg" width="1680" height="945"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption><a href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> secretly meets <a href="/wiki/Illyrio_Mopatis" title="Illyrio Mopatis">Illyrio</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a>.</figcaption></figure><p>Eddard states that this act would be dishonorable, cowardly, and beneath their dignity. The other members of the council: <a href="/wiki/Pycelle" title="Pycelle">Pycelle</a>, Renly, Littlefinger and Varys, all concur that Daenerys must die for the good of the realm. King Robert orders Ned to enact the order. He refuses and resigns as the <a href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a>, to Robert's utter disbelief and fury. </p> <p>Eddard orders his household to begin packing. As Ned is packing, Littlefinger arrives and offers to take Ned to the last place Jon Arryn went before he "fell ill". Ned agrees. </p> <p>Ser Loras and Lord Renly, who are lovers, meet in a bedchamber where Renly complains as Robert is organizing a hunt in the Kingswood, which will no doubt involve traipsing around the forest for a couple of weeks in the cold and rain waiting to kill some animal. Loras urges Renly to claim the crown himself, offering him the allegiance of his <a href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">rich and powerful family</a>. Renly dismisses the notion, since he is fourth in line to the Iron Throne. Loras points out that <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a> is a monster, that <a href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen</a> is only eight and laughs at the notion of Robert and Renly's other brother, Lord <a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a>, ever becoming king, as he has the charisma and personality of a lobster. He also asserts that the people love Renly and want to serve him because he is kind and doesn't revel in violence as his brothers do. </p> <p>Queen <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a> and Robert share a rare drink and discuss the Targaryen threat. Surprisingly, Cersei takes Eddard's side, pointing out that the <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> cannot cross the <a href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a> as they have no ships.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":9}' data-ref="9"><a href="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/a4/Loras_and_Renly.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190844"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Loras and Renly.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/a4/Loras_and_Renly.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110615190844" alt="Loras and Renly.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Ser Loras urges <a href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a> to consider his place in the line of succession.</figcaption></figure><p>Robert agrees, but if they land and the knights of Westeros take shelter behind their castle walls, the Dothraki could simply burn and pillage their way up and down the continent until the <a href="/wiki/Smallfolk" title="Smallfolk">smallfolk</a> accept Viserys as their king regardless. Cersei suggests that the unified armies of the Seven Kingdoms could defeat them but Robert says she underestimates the skill and ability of one army with one leader and one purpose as compared to that of the scattered and patchwork armies of Westeros. Their discussion moves to <a href="/wiki/Lyanna_Stark" title="Lyanna Stark">Lyanna Stark</a>, which surprises Robert, as in seventeen years Cersei has never asked about her. He tells her that Lyanna's death left in him a void that seven kingdoms could not fill. He also tells her that their marriage never had a chance of success because of the hold Lyanna's memory has on him, and bitterly adds he can barely remember what she looked like. Cersei says that she felt something for Robert once, even after they lost <a href="/wiki/Cersei%27s_black-haired_son" title="Cersei's black-haired son" class="mw-redirect">their first boy</a>, for quite a while. She asks Robert if there was ever any chance for their marriage and he answers "no".  </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Littlefinger" title="Littlefinger" class="mw-redirect">Littlefinger</a> brings Eddard Stark to the last place Jon Arryn went before falling ill, which is one of <a href="/wiki/Littlefinger%27s_brothel" title="Littlefinger's brothel">Littefinger's brothels</a>. There Ned meets another one of Robert's bastards, this time a baby <a href="/wiki/Barra" title="Barra">girl</a> and her mother, a blond whore named <a href="/wiki/Mhaegen" title="Mhaegen">Mhaegen</a>. Mhaegen remarks that her child has Robert's nose and "black" hair.
