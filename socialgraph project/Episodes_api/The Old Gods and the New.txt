 <p></p> <p><b>"The Old Gods and the New"</b> is the sixth episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the sixteenth episode of the series overall. It premiered on May 6, 2012. It was written by co-executive producer <a href="/wiki/Vanessa_Taylor" title="Vanessa Taylor">Vanessa Taylor</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/David_Nutter" title="David Nutter">David Nutter</a>. </p> <p>The episode won a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Creative_Arts_Emmy_Award" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Creative Arts Emmy Award">Creative Arts Emmy Award</a> for Make-Up (Non-Prosthetic) in September 2012<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p><a href="/wiki/Arya" title="Arya" class="mw-redirect">Arya</a> has a surprise visitor; <a href="/wiki/Daenerys" title="Daenerys" class="mw-redirect">Dany</a> vows to take what is hers; <a href="/wiki/Joffrey" title="Joffrey" class="mw-redirect">Joffrey</a> meets his subjects; <a href="/wiki/Qhorin_Halfhand" title="Qhorin Halfhand" class="mw-redirect">Qhorin</a> gives <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon</a> a chance to prove himself. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="In_the_North" section="3">In the North</h3> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":3}' data-ref="3"><a href="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/2/2d/TheonApproachesBran.png/revision/latest?cb=20160719184907"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20797%20498'%2F%3E" alt="TheonApproachesBran.png" width="797" height="498"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/2/2d/TheonApproachesBran.png/revision/latest?cb=20160719184907" alt="TheonApproachesBran.png" width="797" height="498"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Theon approaches Bran, then takes over Winterfell.</figcaption></figure><p>At <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">Maester</a> <a href="/wiki/Luwin" title="Luwin">Luwin</a> frantically sends off a messenger <a href="/wiki/Ravens" title="Ravens">raven</a>. A band of raiders from <a href="/wiki/House_Greyjoy" title="House Greyjoy">House Greyjoy</a>, led by <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a>, take over the undefended castle. Theon had lured the defenders away with a feint at <a href="/wiki/Torrhen%27s_Square" title="Torrhen's Square">Torrhen's Square</a> and then infiltrated the castle using grappling claws. Theon tells <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a> to surrender the castle to him to save his people. Bran submits in front of his assembled people. Ser <a href="/wiki/Rodrik_Cassel" title="Rodrik Cassel">Rodrik Cassel</a> is captured by Theon's men as he is returning from Torrhen's Square. He spits in Theon's face, and <a href="/wiki/Dagmer_Cleftjaw" title="Dagmer Cleftjaw" class="mw-redirect">Dagmer Cleftjaw</a> insists that Theon must kill him, or lose face in front of his <a href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> raiders. Theon botches the beheading of Ser Rodrik, requiring several hacks and a kick, horrifying the people of Winterfell. Later that night, <a href="/wiki/Osha" title="Osha">Osha</a> the <a href="/wiki/Free_Folk" title="Free Folk">wildling</a> woman, has sex with Theon, but then sneaks out of his room while he is asleep. Osha frees Bran, <a href="/wiki/Rickon_Stark" title="Rickon Stark">Rickon Stark</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hodor" title="Hodor">Hodor</a>, and their <a href="/wiki/Direwolves" title="Direwolves">direwolves</a> (<a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">Summer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shaggydog" title="Shaggydog">Shaggydog</a>). She smuggles them out of the castle and kills <a href="/wiki/Drennan" title="Drennan">one</a> of Theon's guards in the process. </p> <h3 id="In_the_Westerlands" section="4"> In the Westerlands </h3> <p>At King <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s army camp, Robb chats with <a href="/wiki/Talisa_Stark" title="Talisa Stark">Talisa</a>, who has followed his forces on their campaign. Robb introduces Talisa to his mother <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a>, who has returned from <a href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a> along with <a href="/wiki/Brienne_of_Tarth" title="Brienne of Tarth">Brienne of Tarth</a>. They are interrupted when Lord <a href="/wiki/Roose_Bolton" title="Roose Bolton">Roose Bolton</a> arrives with the terrible news sent by Luwin's last messenger raven. Furious at Theon's betrayal, Robb intends to march his army back to the North to repel the invasion. Roose advises that the few hundred soldiers left scattered in the North can be rallied by his bastard son, <a href="/wiki/Ramsay_Bolton" title="Ramsay Bolton">Ramsay Snow</a>, who is in the North at <a href="/wiki/The_Dreadfort" title="The Dreadfort">the Dreadfort</a>. They could overcome the small Ironborn invasion force. Roose warns that retreating the entire Northern army group would lose everything they have gained. Robb agrees, but insists that Bolton's men take Theon alive so Robb can look him in the eyes and ask "Why?" before personally executing him. </p> <h3 id="In_the_Riverlands" section="5"> In the Riverlands </h3> <p>At <a href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a>, the main base camp for the <a href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a> army in the Riverlands, Tywin Lannister berates Ser <a href="/wiki/Amory_Lorch" title="Amory Lorch">Amory Lorch</a> for delivering a letter to their enemies instead of their allies, because he cannot read properly. He discovers that his <a href="/wiki/Cupbearer" title="Cupbearer">cupbearer</a> "Arry" (secretly <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a>) can read.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":4}' data-ref="4"><a href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/0/0e/JoffreyAttackedSeason2.png/revision/latest?cb=20120507032726"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20797%20498'%2F%3E" alt="JoffreyAttackedSeason2.png" width="797" height="498"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/0/0e/JoffreyAttackedSeason2.png/revision/latest?cb=20120507032726" alt="JoffreyAttackedSeason2.png" width="797" height="498"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Joffrey has excrement thrown on him by rioting peasants.</figcaption></figure><p><a href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr "Littlefinger" Baelish</a> arrives at camp and meets with Tywin. Arya tries to hide her face to prevent Littlefinger from identifying her. Baelish discusses the assassination of <a href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a>, and the subsequent rallying of the noble Houses from <a href="/wiki/The_Stormlands" title="The Stormlands">the Stormlands</a> under the banner of his older brother <a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>. However, <a href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Reach" title="The Reach">the Reach</a> has retreated its large army back to their capital at <a href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a>, and has not joined Stannis. Baelish discusses with Tywin the possibility of forming an alliance between House Lannister and House Tyrell. </p> <p>Later Tywin questions Arya about her origins. While Tywin is distracted, Arya sees that a letter on his table is about her older brother Robb Stark, so she snatches it. After asking Tywin about his own father, Tywin sends her to fetch more firewood. She takes this opportunity to read the letter, but unfortunately bumps into Amory Lorch who sees that she is carrying the letter and grows suspicious. Arya runs away and Amory heads to tell Tywin. Arya runs to find <a href="/wiki/Jaqen_H%27ghar" title="Jaqen H'ghar">Jaqen H'ghar</a>, telling him that Amory Lorch is the second name he owes her. Just as Amory Lorch reaches Tywin's quarters, he falls down dead with a poisoned dart lodged in his neck. </p> <h3 id="In_King.27s_Landing" section="6"> In King's Landing </h3> <p>The royal court including King <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey Baratheon</a>, Queen Regent <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a>, Prince <a href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen Baratheon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa Stark</a> are at the docks seeing off Princess <a href="/wiki/Myrcella_Baratheon" title="Myrcella Baratheon">Myrcella Baratheon</a> as she departs in a ship bound for <a href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a>. Cersei is heartbroken and warns Tyrion that she will not forgive him for sending away her only daughter. </p> <p>As per Tyrion's plans, Myrcella will stay in Dorne with <a href="/wiki/House_Martell" title="House Martell">House Martell</a> until she comes of age to fulfill the marriage alliance to their youngest son, <a href="/wiki/Trystane_Martell" title="Trystane Martell">Trystane Martell</a>. The <a href="/wiki/High_Septon_(Before_the_riot_of_King%27s_Landing)" title="High Septon (Before the riot of King's Landing)">High Septon</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">Faith of the Seven</a> offers a blessing for her departure. The streets of the capital city are overflowing with starving refugees from the <a href="/wiki/War_of_the_Five_Kings" title="War of the Five Kings">War of the Five Kings</a>, as Cersei ignored both Tyrion and Janos Slynt's earlier warnings that something had to be done to care for the starving <a href="/wiki/Smallfolk" title="Smallfolk">peasants</a> or there would be mounting discontent with her son's rule. </p> <p>As the royal party travels back to the <a href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a> through the crowded streets, the starving refugees and commoners of King's Landing plead for bread and hurl insults at King Joffrey, and ultimately, someone throws cow excrement into Joffrey's face. Despite being accompanied by only a few dozen guards facing hundreds of peasants, Joffrey impetuously orders his soldiers to kill the entire crowd.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":5}' data-ref="5"><a href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/c/cf/SansaAlmostRapedSeason2.png/revision/latest?cb=20160720012702"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20797%20498'%2F%3E" alt="SansaAlmostRapedSeason2.png" width="797" height="498"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/c/cf/SansaAlmostRapedSeason2.png/revision/latest?cb=20160720012702" alt="SansaAlmostRapedSeason2.png" width="797" height="498"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Sansa is almost raped.</figcaption></figure><p>A <a href="/wiki/Riot_of_King%27s_Landing" title="Riot of King's Landing">riot</a> subsequently erupts and spreads throughout the city. The royal party's guards are soon overwhelmed, and Joffrey, Cersei and Tyrion barely make it back to the castle unscathed. The High Septon is literally torn apart by the mob. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> is enraged by Joffrey's shocking stupidity, for massively overreacting to having cow dung thrown at him, and for not realizing that his orders would cause a riot among the starving peasants and endanger the entire royal party. Joffrey, still manically cursing the mob and demanding they all be killed, shrieks that Tyrion cannot speak to him in that way and petulantly reminds his uncle that he is talking to a king. Tyrion then contemptuously slaps his nephew across the face but Joffrey does not retaliate. The scant two thousand <a href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a> guards have difficulty containing the riot, and chaos reigns throughout the city. Tyrion realizes to his horror that Sansa Stark was lost in the crowd. Joffrey petulantly refuses to send his men after her, oblivious to her importance as a <a href="/wiki/Hostage" title="Hostage">hostage</a> or the fact that if any harm comes to her, the Starks may retaliate by executing his uncle <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime</a>. Outside, Sansa is dragged away by a mob of angry men and is about to be raped, when <a href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor "The Hound" Clegane</a> arrives and slays her assailants. He carries Sansa to the castle and orders handmaids to take care of her.
