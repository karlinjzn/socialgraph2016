For the actual highway, see "<a href="/wiki/Kingsroad" title="Kingsroad">Kingsroad</a>".</i> </dd></dl> <p><b>"The Kingsroad"</b> is the second episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the second episode of the series overall. It first aired on April 24, 2011. It was written by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Tim_Van_Patten" title="Tim Van Patten">Tim Van Patten</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":2}' data-ref="2"><a href="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/68/Kingsroad3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110425140718"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Kingsroad3.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/68/Kingsroad3.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110425140718" alt="Kingsroad3.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption><a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> prepares to depart for <a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>.</figcaption></figure><p>The <a href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannisters</a> plot to ensure <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran</a>'s silence; <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> leave for the<a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall"> Wall</a>, and along the way Jon discovers his ideas of the <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> may not match the reality. <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Ned</a> faces a family crisis en route to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="Across_the_Narrow_Sea" section="3">Across the Narrow Sea</h3> <p>The <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a></i> led by <a href="/wiki/Khal" title="Khal">Khal</a> <a href="/wiki/Drogo" title="Drogo">Drogo</a> have departed <a href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a> and are heading east. They travel away from the <a href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a> and into the <a href="/wiki/Dothraki_sea" title="Dothraki sea" class="mw-redirect">Dothraki sea</a>, the long-grassed plains that the Dothraki call home. <a href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys Targaryen</a> accompanies the <i>khalasar</i>, meaning to stay with Drogo until he fulfills his end of the bargain and provides him with an army with which to invade <a href="/wiki/Westeros" title="Westeros">Westeros</a>, despite Ser <a href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a>'s suggestion. He tells Viserys that he offended his liege lord, <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>, by selling poachers into <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slavery</a>. When he received word that Eddard meant to execute him for the crime (slavery has been illegal in Westeros for centuries), he fled to <a href="/wiki/Essos" title="Essos">Essos</a>. Viserys laughs at the notion, saying such trivialities would not be punished under his reign. <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a> is finding adapting to her new role as <i><a href="/wiki/Khaleesi" title="Khaleesi">khaleesi</a></i> to be a huge challenge. Only Mormont offers her some solace,telling her it will become easier. Daenerys is however fearful of her new husband and it is revealed that their first sexual encounter has left her slightly injured. </p> <p>Fascinated with her gift from <a href="/wiki/Illyrio_Mopatis" title="Illyrio Mopatis">Illyrio</a>, Dany asks her handmaidens if they have ever heard of dragons surviving in the east. They reply no, but <a href="/wiki/Doreah" title="Doreah">Doreah</a>, a former pleasure-girl from <a href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lys</a>, tells her an old story about how there used to be two moons in the sky. One wandered too close to the sun and cracked like an egg, spilling out thousands of dragons into the world. Dany's Dothraki handmaidens <a href="/wiki/Irri" title="Irri">Irri</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jhiqui" title="Jhiqui">Jhiqui</a> dismiss the story, saying that the moon is a goddess, wife to the sun. </p> <p>Daenerys seeks the advice of her handmaid Doreah. Doreah instructs Daenerys on how to please Drogo in bed but also how to use her skills to gain respect from him. Reminding her, "you are no slave" and should not have sex like one. When Joffrey refuses, saying the boy means nothing to him, Tyrion slaps him three times until he accepts his uncle's suggestion. Tyrion then breakfasts with his other family members, informing them of his decision to journey north to see <a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> before returning to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. He also tells them that Bran is expected to live and notes <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime</a>'s guarded reactions. </p> <p>Cersei visits <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a>, sitting by her son's side, and tells her that <a href="/wiki/Cersei%27s_black-haired_son" title="Cersei's black-haired son" class="mw-redirect">her first child</a>, a beautiful, black-haired boy, died of a fever. She and Robert were grief-stricken and her prayers and tears were for naught. She offers to pray for Bran's survival, hoping that this time <a href="/wiki/Faith_of_the_Seven" title="Faith of the Seven">the gods</a> will listen. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> has <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>'s blacksmith, <a href="/wiki/Mikken" title="Mikken">Mikken</a>, forge a light sword for his sister, <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a>. Arya is excited by the gift and names it <a href="/wiki/Needle" title="Needle">Needle</a>. Jon also gives her advice on how to best use the weapon: "Stick them with the pointy end." Jon urges Arya to practice every chance she gets. Jon then says goodbye to the comatose Bran, before departing at his stepmother's cold request. Eddard then says his goodbyes to his son and to Catelyn, who is angered at his departure while Bran remains ill.        </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":4}' data-ref="4"><a href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/2/2b/Distant_Winterfell.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110305210409"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Distant Winterfell.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/2/2b/Distant_Winterfell.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110305210409" alt="Distant Winterfell.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>The two parties depart <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>.</figcaption></figure><p>The parties depart Winterfell: Jon, Tyrion and <a href="/wiki/Benjen_Stark" title="Benjen Stark">Benjen Stark</a> to <a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>; and Eddard, Arya, <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a> and the royal party south to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. At their parting Eddard tells Jon that, though he does not have Eddard's name, he has his blood. He also promises Jon to tell him about his mother the next time they meet.        </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":5}' data-ref="5"><a href="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/63/Eddard_and_Jon.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110315133744"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Eddard and Jon.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette1.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/6/63/Eddard_and_Jon.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110315133744" alt="Eddard and Jon.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption><a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> says goodbye to his <a href="/wiki/Bastard" title="Bastard" class="mw-redirect">bastard</a> son.</figcaption></figure><p>Days later, on the <a href="/wiki/Kingsroad" title="Kingsroad">Kingsroad</a> south to the capital, <a href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">King Robert Baratheon</a> calls a halt to discuss with Eddard some dire news that has just arrived. Robert's spies have learned of Daenerys Targaryen's marriage to Khal Drogo, whose <i>khalasar</i> is reported to number over one hundred thousand warriors. Eddard points out that the <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> cannot cross the <a href="/wiki/Narrow_Sea" title="Narrow Sea">Narrow Sea</a>, as they have no ships, but Robert is concerned that the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a> will soon face another war. A curious Robert asks Eddard about <a href="/wiki/Wylla" title="Wylla">Wylla</a>, the mother of his bastard son, but Eddard refuses to enlighten him. Robert reminds him that at that time, they were in the middle of a war and no one knew if they would ever return to their families. </p> <p>In Winterfell, Catelyn's refusal to leave Bran's side is starting to cause problems for the smooth running of the castle. <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb</a> offers to work with <a href="/wiki/Maesters" title="Maesters">Maester</a> <a href="/wiki/Luwin" title="Luwin">Luwin</a> to put the castle to rights. There is then a fire in part of the castle, but this proves to be a distraction to allow <a href="/wiki/Catspaw_assassin" title="Catspaw assassin">an assassin</a> to attempt to finish off Bran. Catelyn holds the assassin off long enough for <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">Bran's direwolf</a> to enter the chamber and tear out the assassin's throat. </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":6}' data-ref="6"><a href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/4/48/Kingsroad1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160720020446"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Kingsroad1.jpg" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/4/48/Kingsroad1.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160720020446" alt="Kingsroad1.jpg" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Jon and <a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> discussing matters.</figcaption></figure><p>Traveling north, Jon and Tyrion talk, and Tyrion reveals that he reads books to sharpen his mind, his greatest asset and weapon. As it travels north, the party is joined by several, new recruits — rapists who chose to "take the black," rather than face the knife. Tyrion tells Jon that the Night's Watch is not a noble institution of glorious knights defending the realm against the <a href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">horrors</a> lurking beyond the Wall, but a dumping ground for the unwanted and for criminals. </p> <p>In Winterfell, Catelyn goes to the tower from where Bran fell and finds a strand of blonde hair on the floor. She summons Robb, <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon</a>, Luwin and the castle master at arms, Ser <a href="/wiki/Rodrik_Cassel" title="Rodrik Cassel">Rodrik Cassel</a> and tells them that she suspects that the <a href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">Lannisters</a> tried to kill Bran because he might have seen something in the tower. Robb and Theon are all for making war, but Luwin schools them to caution. Catelyn decides that Ned must know the truth and resolves to travel south to King's Landing and meet him there. Ser Rodrik will go as escort, while Robb will take charge of the castle in her absence.        </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":7}' data-ref="7"><a href="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/9/97/The_Wall_from_the_south.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20150323201042"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201920%201080'%2F%3E" alt="The Wall from the south.jpg" width="1920" height="1080"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/9/97/The_Wall_from_the_south.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20150323201042" alt="The Wall from the south.jpg" width="1920" height="1080"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption><a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">The Wall</a>, as seen by Jon's party.</figcaption></figure><p>Jon's party crests a rise and sees the Wall before them. Benjen welcomes them, and Tyrion seems impressed by the Wall's sheer size. </p> <p>On the Kingsroad, the royal party reaches the <a href="/wiki/Crossroads_Inn" title="Crossroads Inn" class="mw-redirect">Crossroads Inn</a>, a noted waystop on the way south to the capital. Prince Joffrey's bodyguard, <a href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a>, also known as "the Hound," a man with half his face burned in a mysterious accident, introduces Sansa to <a href="/wiki/Ilyn_Payne" title="Ilyn Payne">Ser Ilyn Payne</a>, the king's headsman, a frightening-looking man who had his tongue torn out on the orders of the <a href="/wiki/Aerys_II_Targaryen" title="Aerys II Targaryen">Mad King</a> many years ago. Joffrey saves Sansa from this unpleasantness and takes her for a walk along the riverbank. They find Arya practicing her sword work with the son of the party's butcher, <a href="/wiki/Mycah" title="Mycah">Mycah</a>. Joffrey pretends to take offense at Mycah using a sword on the sister of his betrothed and starts to gleefully cut Mycah's face with his sword, but an outraged Arya hits him on the head with a stick. Joffrey quickly becomes violent and threatens Arya at sword point, but before he can hurt her, Arya's direwolf, <a href="/wiki/Nymeria_(direwolf)" title="Nymeria (direwolf)">Nymeria</a>, attacks Joffrey, biting at his arm. Mycah runs off, and Arya throws Joffrey's sword in the river before running off herself. Sansa tries to help Joffrey, but he spitefully tells her to leave him alone and go for help. </p> <p>Arya, knowing that Nymeria will be punished for injuring the prince, forces the direwolf to flee by throwing a rock at her. She is then taken before the King, who is angered that a minor fracas has become a major incident, with his wife and new <a href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand</a> blaming one another's children. He decides to let Ned discipline Arya while he will do the same to Joffrey, but Cersei demands that the direwolf be executed. When Nymeria cannot be found, Cersei spitefully requests that Sansa's direwolf <a href="/wiki/Lady" title="Lady">Lady</a> be killed instead, and Robert acquiesces, upsetting Sansa and angering Arya. </p> <p>Furious, Eddard attends to the matter himself, passing the Hound arriving with Mycah's bloodied corpse. Eddard is shocked and disgusted, and when he asks if the Hound ran Mycah down, the Hound responds "He ran, but not very fast." Eddard kills Lady with a dagger, and hundreds of miles away, Bran awakens. </p> <h2 id="Recap" section="5">Recap</h2> <dl><dd> <i>Main: <a href="/wiki/The_Kingsroad_recap" title="The Kingsroad The series marks his first acting role. </li> </ul> <h2 id="Notes" section="12">Notes</h2> <ul> <li> The episode takes its name from the <a href="/wiki/Kingsroad" title="Kingsroad">Kingsroad</a>, a massive road which runs from <a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a> to <a href="/wiki/Storm%27s_End" title="Storm's End">Storm's End</a>. </li> <li> A notable change from the books is that the events at the Crossroads Inn actually happen at Castle Darry, a small holdfast south of the river. <a href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a> and Ser <a href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Barristan Selmy</a> also first appear here in the books, journeying north with Ser Ilyn Payne to meet the King's party. In the TV series only Ser Ilyn appears, and the other two remain in King's Landing until the third episode. </li> <li> First mention of <a href="/wiki/Wylla" title="Wylla">Wylla</a>, the alleged mother of Jon Snow. </li> <li> The title sequence changes in this episode, dropping <a href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a> from the map and incorporating <a href="/wiki/Vaes_Dothrak" title="Vaes Dothrak">Vaes Dothrak</a>, although it will be several episodes until Daenerys and the Dothraki get there. The title sequence is the first time that Vaes Dothrak and the surrounding region has been depicted on a map, being absent from both the maps in the novels and also on the HBO website. The location of Vaes Dothrak in the sequence is erroneous though as later maps show the city lying in farther in the east of Essos, while the title sequence map shows it much closer to the center of the Dothraki sea. </li> <li>According to episode writer <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Cogman" title="Bryan Cogman">Bryan Cogman</a>, episodes of <i>Game of Thrones</i> are only named late into their production, after principal photography is completed. The first title that Cogman pitched to David Benioff for this episode was "A Direwolf is No Pet" (which is a quote from the episode). Benioff responded by making Cogman promise that if he were ever to suddenly die, Cogman would never under any circumstances use the title "A Direwolf is No Pet" for any episodes of the entire series. Ultimately Cogman came up with "The Kingsroad" as the name for this episode.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup> </li> <li>In the novels, <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei</a> never gave birth to a child of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert</a>. She told <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> that she was once pregnant with a child of Robert, but had the child aborted out of hatred for him. Robert was unaware of both the pregnancy and the abortion. Cersei isn't simply lying in this episode to manipulate Catelyn, as in a later episode (<a href="/wiki/The_Wolf_and_the_Lion" title="The Wolf and the Lion">The Wolf and the Lion</a>) she discusses with Robert their first child, who was "black of hair" like Robert. </li> <li>Tyrion says that his father Tywin was Hand of the King for twenty years, to which Jon responds "Until your brother killed that king," which Tyrion acknowledges. This is actually a condensed version of what happened: Tywin was Hand for twenty years, but he resigned in protest a few years before the rebellion started, so he wasn't actually Hand when Aerys died. This is not a difference between the books and TV series, the characters are just speaking loosely: other references to these events in the TV continuity specify that Tywin resigned just before the rebellion. </li> <li>When Joffrey offers Sansa more wine, she says that she shouldn't, as her father only lets his children have a cup of wine at special feasts. In the books, during the feast at Winterfell (which happened last episode), Jon Snow is the POV narrator, and he notes that Ned Stark does indeed only let his children have a single cup of wine at feasts. In the books, Jon does attend the feast for the king but is instead seated elsewhere in the great hall. While the books note he usually sits at the table with his family, Jon is instead placed among younger squires at the other end of the great hall during this occasion. Jon doesn't mind (apart from the usual hurt at being rejected by Catelyn) because, as he is unsupervised due to not being at his father's table in this instance, he can drink as much wine as he wants and, indeed, gets rather drunk. However, his Uncle Benjen later catches him drunk in this chapter. </li> <li>In the first episode, Iain Glen's name in the title credits is accompanied by a square with a neutral pattern. In this episode this is corrected as his name appears beside a square depicting a bear for house Mormont. </li> </ul> <h2 id="Commentary" section="13">Commentary</h2> <p>Actors Mark Addy, Lena Heady and Nikolaj Coster-Waldau provide a commentary for the episode on Blu-ray and DVD (Mark and Lena together in London, Nikolaj on a video link from Copenhagen). This commentary is more humorous than the producers' commentary for the previous episode. </p> <ul> <li> Lena Headey and Mark Addy observe that the opening title sequence does a good job of orienting people within the world of Westeros. Lena proceeds to give an impersonation of locations that could be used by a Westeros-based satnav device. </li> <li> None of the three actors were involved in any of the shooting on Malta or the scenes involving the Dothraki, so were fascinated to see how Daenerys's story unfolded. </li> <li> Lena Headey finds the relationship between Ser Jorah and Daenerys fascinating. </li> <li> Lena Headey suggests that Mark Addy could return in future seasons as Robert Baratheon's ghost. </li> <li> All three actors find Peter Dinklage 'irritatingly great.' </li> <li> The scene where Cersei talks about her dying first son was Lena Headey's audition piece. Lena's take on Cersei is that when she was very young she was not unlike Sansa. </li> <li> Nikolaj Coster-Waldau mis-identifies Mikken as being played by Tommy Dunne, when in fact he is played by Boyd Rankin. Mark Addy then mentions Boyd Rankin's behind-the-scenes role on the show. Nikolaj's take on Jaime Lannister's view of Jon Snow and the Night's Watch is that he is completely baffled by the very idea of the organization. </li> <li> All three actors think the difficult relationship between Jon Snow and Catelyn to be extremely well-played. </li> <li> Mark Addy and Sean Bean were previously acquainted from drama school (though they were in different years), as well as working on the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Riding_Trilogy" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Red Riding Trilogy">Red Riding</a></i> TV movie trilogy a couple of years earlier. </li> <li> Sean Bean was noted on set for stealing other people's sandwiches. </li> <li> Jaime Lannister was supposed to be present in the background of the scene where Eddard and Robert discuss Jon Snow's mother and Daenerys's wedding, but the schedule didn't allow for it. The scene between Jaime and Jory was written to make up for Nikolaj Coster-Waldau's reduction in screentime in this scene. </li> <li> The three actors agree that Eddard Stark's storyline is one of constantly making bad decisions. </li> <li> Nikolaj Coster-Waldau notes the irony of Jon Snow's disapproval of Jaime breaking his oath to kill the Mad King (in his discussion with Tyrion in the woods) given his later decision to break his oath to the Night's Watch in the <a href="/wiki/Fire_and_Blood" title="Fire and Blood">final episode</a> of the season. </li> <li> The wolves had a tendency to wake up the actors early in the morning with a chorus of howling. </li> <li> Lena Headey considers the costumes to be brilliant, but finds some of the helmets to be veering a little towards Monty Python. </li> <li> Lena Headey and Nikolaj Coster-Waldau love the phrase 'twincest,' which fans coined some years earlier to describe Cersei and Jaime's relationship. </li> <li> Nikolaj Coster-Waldau expands on the scene from the first episode where Jon, Theon and Robb were shaved. Apparently Alfie Allen refused to eat for two days prior to the scene to ensure he looked as good as possible. </li> <li> During the scene where Doreah tutors Daenerys in the arts of love, Nikolaj Coster-Waldau observes the accuracy of the fan term 'sexposition' to describe such scenes. The three actors then debate whether Daenerys is indeed suffering from a form of <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stockholm_Syndrome" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Stockholm Syndrome">Stockholm Syndrome</a> in her relationship with Khal Drogo. </li> <li> Lena Headey is a fan of the character development of Sansa throughout the first season, particularly her movement from being 'wet' to being stronger in her scenes with Joffrey in the final episode of the season. </li> <li> The actors note that because there were so many storylines progressing in which they were not involved, it was hard to see how the whole thing would come together until they saw the finished series. </li> </ul> <h2 id="In_the_books" section="14">In the books</h2> <dl><dd> <i>See: <a href="/wiki/Differences_between_books_and_TV_series_-_Season_1#The_Kingsroad" title="Differences between books and TV series - Season 1">Differences between books and TV series - Season 1#The Kingsroad</a> </i> </dd></dl> <ul><li> The episode is adapted from the following chapters of <i>A Game of Thrones</i>: <ul> <li> Chapter 9, Tyrion I. </li> <li> Chapter 10, Jon II. </li> <li> Chapter 12, Eddard II. </li> <li> Chapter 13, Tyrion II. </li> <li> Chapter 14, Catelyn III. </li> <li> Chapter 15, Sansa I. </li> <li> Chapter 16, Eddard III. </li> <li> Chapter 17, Bran III. </li> <li> Chapter 19, Jon III. </li> <li> Chapter 23, Daenerys III. </li> </ul> </li></ul> <h2 id="Memorable_quotes" section="15">Memorable quotes<br> </h2> <p><a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon"><b>Joffrey Baratheon</b></a>: "Better-looking bitches than you're used to, Uncle.
