 <p></p> <p><b>"The Night Lands"</b> is the second episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the twelfth episode of the series overall. It premiered on April 8, 2012. It was written by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> and directed by co-executive producer <a href="/wiki/Alan_Taylor" title="Alan Taylor">Alan Taylor</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p><a href="/wiki/Stannis" title="Stannis" class="mw-redirect">Stannis</a> uses Ser <a href="/wiki/Davos" title="Davos" class="mw-redirect">Davos</a> to seek out new allies for his war with the <a href="/wiki/Lannisters" title="Lannisters" class="mw-redirect">Lannisters</a>. On the road north, <a href="/wiki/Arya" title="Arya" class="mw-redirect">Arya</a> confides in <a href="/wiki/Gendry" title="Gendry">Gendry</a>. <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a> sends <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> to win an alliance with his <a href="/wiki/Balon_Greyjoy" title="Balon Greyjoy">father</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">fierce warriors</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Islands" title="Iron Islands">Iron Islands</a>. <a href="/wiki/Cersei" title="Cersei" class="mw-redirect">Cersei</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tyrion" title="Tyrion" class="mw-redirect">Tyrion</a> clash on how to rule in <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="In_the_Seven_Kingdoms" section="3">In the Seven Kingdoms</h3> <p>In <a href="/wiki/The_Riverlands" title="The Riverlands">the Riverlands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> continues to pose as a boy while traveling north with <a href="/wiki/Yoren" title="Yoren">Yoren</a> and his <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> recruits, and urinates privately. She is drawn to the caged prisoners (<a href="/wiki/Jaqen_H%27ghar" title="Jaqen H'ghar">Jaqen H'ghar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Rorge" title="Rorge">Rorge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Biter" title="Biter">Biter</a>) when Jaqen asks her for water and introduces himself. A pair of <a href="/wiki/Gold_Cloaks" title="Gold Cloaks" class="mw-redirect">Gold Cloaks</a> approach the party and Arya hides, believing that they have come for her. They deliver a warrant for the Royal bastard <a href="/wiki/Gendry" title="Gendry">Gendry</a> but <a href="/wiki/Yoren" title="Yoren">Yoren</a> intimidates them into leaving empty handed. They promise to return with more men. </p> <p>Arya asks Gendry why he is wanted and he admits that he does not know. He tells her that he was questioned by Lord <a href="/wiki/Jon_Arryn" title="Jon Arryn">Jon Arryn</a> and Lord <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> in the months before he fled <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. He questions why she thought that they had come for her and tells her that he has seen through her disguise. Arya reveals her identity to him and swears him to secrecy. </p> <p>In King's Landing, <a href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> discovers <a href="/wiki/Shae" title="Shae">Shae</a> living with <a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Tower_of_the_Hand" title="Tower of the Hand">Tower of the Hand</a> and makes implied threats about revealing her presence. Tyrion warns Varys not to underestimate him. <a href="/wiki/Alton_Lannister" title="Alton Lannister">Alton Lannister</a> delivers King <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s peace terms to the <a href="/wiki/Small_council" title="Small council" class="mw-redirect">small council</a> and Queen Regent <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a> rejects them. <a href="/wiki/Grand_Maester" title="Grand Maester">Grand Maester</a> <a href="/wiki/Pycelle" title="Pycelle">Pycelle</a> relays a message from the <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> requesting manpower and warning of <a href="/wiki/Wights" title="Wights">wights</a>. Tyrion is the only one to take the warning seriously. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Ros" title="Ros">Ros</a> struggles to continue her work as a <a href="/wiki/Prostitution" title="Prostitution">prostitute</a> having witnessed the murder of the infant royal <a href="/wiki/Bastardy" title="Bastardy">bastard</a> <a href="/wiki/Barra" title="Barra">Barra</a>. <a href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a> threatens her with a tale about selling an unhappy girl to a depraved customer in order to recoup his investment. He orders her to return happy after a night off. </p> <p>Tyrion has Lord <a href="/wiki/Janos_Slynt" title="Janos Slynt">Janos Slynt</a> for dinner and questions him about the <a href="/wiki/Massacre_in_King%27s_Landing" title="Massacre in King's Landing">massacre of the royal bastards</a>. Janos freely admits his involvement but will not reveal who ordered the attack. Tyrion has Janos arrested and exiled to the <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> and installs <a href="/wiki/Bronn" title="Bronn">Bronn</a> as <a href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Commander</a> of the <a href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a> in his place. Cersei confronts Tyrion about his actions. He warns that she is losing the support of the people but she claims that she does not care. He concludes that <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a> ordered the massacre, having initially suspected that it was Cersei. He jokes about her relationship with <a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime</a> and she says that he has always been funny. She ends the discussion with the barb that his finest joke was killing their mother when he was born. </p> <p>On <a href="/wiki/Dragonstone" title="Dragonstone" class="mw-redirect">Dragonstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Davos_Seaworth" title="Davos Seaworth">Davos</a> recruits the <a href="/wiki/Lys" title="Lys">Lysene</a> <a href="/wiki/Sellsail" title="Sellsail" class="mw-redirect">sellsail</a> <a href="/wiki/Salladhor_Saan" title="Salladhor Saan">Salladhor Saan</a> and his fleet of thirty ships to King <a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>'s cause. His son <a href="/wiki/Matthos_Seaworth" title="Matthos Seaworth">Matthos Seaworth</a> encourages him to learn to read so that he can study the holy texts of the <a href="/wiki/R%27hllor" title="R'hllor">Lord of Light</a> but Davos is happy to put his faith solely in Stannis. <a href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">Melisandre</a> seduces <a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis</a> with the promise of a son. At first, Stannis hesitates claiming he has a <a href="/wiki/Selyse_Baratheon" title="Selyse Baratheon">wife</a> but Melisandre still manages to successfully seduce Stannis. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> travels to the <a href="/wiki/Iron_Islands" title="Iron Islands">Iron Islands</a> on a merchant ship and sleeps with the <a href="/wiki/Captain%27s_daughter" title="Captain's daughter">Captain's daughter</a>. He tells her that he is expecting a warm reception and is crushed when there is no one to meet him when he disembarks in <a href="/wiki/Lordsport" title="Lordsport">Lordsport</a>. A woman offers him a ride to <a href="/wiki/Pyke_(castle)" title="Pyke (castle)">Pyke</a> and he attempts to seduce her throughout the journey. He is further disappointed when his father <a href="/wiki/Balon_Greyjoy" title="Balon Greyjoy">Balon</a> questions his loyalty and his memory of his roots having spent nine years as a ward of <a href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a>. He delivers <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>'s letter suggesting an alliance against <a href="/wiki/House_Lannister" title="House Lannister">House Lannister</a>, for which Robb will make Balon King of the Iron Islands again. His guide enters and he is shocked to realize that she is his sister <a href="/wiki/Yara_Greyjoy" title="Yara Greyjoy">Yara</a> when Balon greets her warmly. Balon rejects the proposal and compares Theon unfavourably to his sister. He insists that he will follow the <a href="/wiki/Ironborn" title="Ironborn">ironborn</a> tradition of paying the <a href="/wiki/Iron_price" title="Iron price">iron price</a> (meaning to take something through force) and says that no one will give him his crown. When Theon warns his father that he will not defeat the Lannisters, Balon retorts that he has no intention of fighting the Lannisters. </p> <h3 id="Across_the_Narrow_Sea" section="4">Across the Narrow Sea</h3> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":2}' data-ref="2"><a href="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/7/7c/Daenerys_202.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20120221232427"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%203888%202592'%2F%3E" alt="Daenerys 202.jpg" width="3888" height="2592"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/7/7c/Daenerys_202.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20120221232427" alt="Daenerys 202.jpg" width="3888" height="2592"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Daenerys waits for news</figcaption></figure><p><a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> and her <a href="/wiki/Khalasar" title="Khalasar">khalasar</a> lie exhausted in the heat, their water dwindling as they wait for the return of her <a href="/wiki/Bloodriders" title="Bloodriders" class="mw-redirect">bloodriders</a>. <a href="/wiki/Rakharo" title="Rakharo">Rakharo</a>'s horse returns riderless and Ser <a href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a> discovers his head and severed braid in its saddle bag. He surmises that Rakharo was caught and killed by a rival khalasar. <a href="/wiki/Irri" title="Irri">Irri</a> is devastated by the death and believes the mutilation will prevent Rakharo from entering the <a href="/wiki/Night_Lands" title="Night Lands">Night Lands</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> land of the dead. Daenerys promises to hold a funeral for Rakharo. </p> <h3 id="Beyond_the_Wall" section="5">Beyond the Wall</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> rescues one of <a href="/wiki/Craster" title="Craster">Craster</a>'s wives, <a href="/wiki/Gilly" title="Gilly">Gilly</a>, from <a href="/wiki/Ghost" title="Ghost">Ghost</a> when he bothers her for food. She confides her desire to flee from the keep because of her fear for what Craster will do if her unborn child is a boy. Sam takes her to Jon and she begs him to take her with the ranging but will not say what she is afraid of. <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon</a> refuses to aid her because of the <a href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Lord Commander</a>'s orders not to get involved with Craster's wives. </p> <p>Jon notices Craster taking a <a href="/wiki/Craster%27s_last_son" title="Craster's last son">baby</a> into the woods at night. He follows him and sees him leave the child to the <a href="/wiki/White_Walkers" title="White Walkers">White Walkers</a>. Before he can intervene Craster surprises him and knocks him out. </p> <h2 id="Recap" section="6">Recap</h2> <dl><dd> <i>Main: <a href="/wiki/The_Night_Lands_recap" title="The Night Lands recap">The the dead in <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> religion. Irri is devastated that Rakharo will not go to the Night Lands because his body was mutilated by his killers without being burned. </li> <li>The scene in which Rakharo's severed head is discovered and Irri mourns his death (in a protracted exchange of <a href="/wiki/Dothraki_(language)" title="Dothraki (language)">Dothraki language</a> dialogue) was filmed as part of the next episode, "What is Dead May Never Die." It was moved to this episode in post-production.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup> </li> <li>Tyrion's removal of Janos Slynt is not entirely of his own initiative: in the books, his father Tywin considered it one of Cersei's many follies that she handed out the drastically disproportionate reward of a seat on the Small Council and lordship of Harrenhal to a lowborn and infamously corrupt man like Janos. Thus, Cersei can't overturn the decision to remove him, because Tyrion had explicit orders from Tywin to do so, which also berated Cersei for elevating Janos in the first place. However, it was Tyrion's decision to not only strip him of his title but exile him to the Wall, because he felt he was of questionable loyalty and because he was disgusted that Janos was a baby-killer. </li> <li>A screenshot of Robb Stark's letter to Balon Greyjoy, which he sent Theon to deliver, was posted in the In-Episode Guide for this episode. The visible portions read: </li> </ul> <dl><dd><dl> <dd>...House Greyjoy Lord of the... </dd> <dd>...years ago our houses fought as enemies... </dd> <dd>...unite them against a greator foe. Give him command of your fleet to crush the Lannisters and I will give you... </dd> <dd>independence you sought some nine years ago... </dd> <dd>shall be king of the Iron Islands as I am King... </dd> <dd>North, free of the oppression of the [Iron Throne]. </dd> <dd>The Lannisters have left their... </dd> <dd>...[Casterly Roc]k largely undefended and... </dd> <dd>Theon speaks for....<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup> </dd> </dl></dd></dl> <ul> <li>As writer <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Cogman" title="Bryan Cogman">Bryan Cogman</a> explained, the production team wasn't sure in Season 2 if they would eventually be able to include Stannis's daughter <a href="/wiki/Shireen_Baratheon" title="Shireen Baratheon">Shireen Baratheon</a> in the TV series. Thus a considerable amount of thought actually went into Melisandre's line in this episode that Stannis has "no <i>sons,</i>" because they wanted to leave their options open to later say that he does have a daughter (and as it turned out, the TV series was ultimately able to introduce Shireen in Season 3): <ul><li>"At that point, we honestly didn't know if we would include Shireen or not — we never know how many characters this show can handle so we sometimes hedge one way or the other. In the books, Ser <a href="/wiki/Alliser_Thorne" title="Alliser Thorne">Alliser Thorne</a> was actually sent to King's Landing to plead for help in person, but he was ignored and sent back - this is one of the reasons why Ser Alliser doesn't take part in the Great Ranging (and ultimately isn't seen again until Castle Black is seen again in Season 4).
