 <p></p> <p><b>"Lord Snow"</b> is the third episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_1" title="Season 1">first season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the third episode of the series overall. It first aired on May 1, 2011. It was written by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/Brian_Kirk" title="Brian Kirk">Brian Kirk</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p><a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> attempts to find his footing at <a href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a> amidst hostility and suspicion from his fellow members of the <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a> and his daughters arrive at <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn</a> renews an old friendship with a member of the court, while <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viserys_Targaryen" title="Viserys Targaryen">Viserys</a> journey onwards to the <a href="/wiki/Dothraki" title="Dothraki">Dothraki</a> capital of <a href="/wiki/Alliser_Thorne" title="Alliser Thorne">Alliser Thorne</a>. </li> <li> This episode is dedicated to the memory of <a href="/wiki/Margaret_John" title="Margaret John">Margaret John</a>, who plays Old Nan, and who sadly passed away on 2 February 2011 after a brief illness. </li> <li> During the Small Council scene, Renly hands Ned Stark a scroll containing the announcement of the tournament and the prizes for the winners, which he then reads off as "40,000 <a href="/wiki/Currency" title="Currency">Gold Dragons</a> for the champion, 20,000 for the runner-up, and 20,000 for the best archer". Pycelle then asks if the crown can afford 80,000 Gold Dragons in prize money. If you pause when Ned first unrolls the scroll, its contents are actually clearly legible: it goes on to say that there is a 20,000 Gold Dragon prize for "Combat with Swords and Clubs", which makes for a total tournament debt of 100,000 Gold Dragons. It is unclear why this discrepancy occurred. </li> <li>The <a href="/wiki/Dothraki_(language)" title="Dothraki (language)">Dothraki</a> word that Irri is heard teaching Daenerys, "<i>athjahakar</i>", is the Dothraki word for "pride". It is derived from "<i>jahak</i>", the long braid worn by Dothraki warriors which they take great pride in, as they only cut their braids when defeated. </li> <li>When the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Steward" title="Royal Steward">Royal Steward</a> greets Eddard Stark and says that Grand Maester Pycelle has called a meeting of the Small Council, the steward mispronounces "maester" as "my-stir"; the correct pronunciation is actually "may-stir". This was reportedly a common mistake on set which took the cast some time to get right. </li> <li>When Joffrey complains to Cersei that every lord in the realm commands his own soldiers raised from his feudal lands, he comments that he feels this is primitive, "no better than the <a href="/wiki/Hill_tribes" title="Hill tribes">Hill tribes</a>." This is the first mention of the hill tribes of the <a href="/wiki/Mountains_of_the_Moon" title="Mountains of the Moon">Mountains of the Moon</a> which ring <a href="/wiki/The_Vale_of_Arryn" title="The Vale of Arryn">the Vale of Arryn</a>, though they won't be encountered on-screen until two episodes after this, in "<a href="/wiki/The_Wolf_and_the_Lion" title="The Wolf and the Lion">The Wolf and the Lion</a>". </li> <li>Joffrey also mentions "uncle Kevan": <a href="/wiki/Kevan_Lannister" title="Kevan Lannister">Kevan Lannister</a> first appears on-screen five episodes from this in "<a href="/wiki/The_Pointy_End" title="The Pointy End">The Pointy End</a>". Of course, Joffrey is speaking loosely, as Kevan is actually his mother's uncle and therefore his great-uncle (it's common in real-life to refer to great-uncles as just "uncle"). </li> <li> <a href="/wiki/Old_Nan" title="Old Nan">Old Nan</a> offers to tell Bran a story about Ser <a href="/wiki/Duncan_the_Tall" title="Duncan the Tall">Duncan the Tall</a>. Duncan, also known as "Dunk", is one of the main characters in the <i>Tales of Dunk and Egg</i> prequel novellas, which take place 89 years before the events of <i>Game of Thrones</i> (there are time skips between novellas in the series, and George R.R. Martin has said that he wants to continue them all the way up to the deaths of the characters, about 50 years before <i>Game of Thrones</i>). Ser Duncan visited Winterfell in one of his adventures, and it is speculated that Old Nan met him in her youth. </li> <li>The scene between Robert, Barristan, and Jaime, the scene between Tyrion, Yoren, and Benjen, and the scene between Jorah, Rakharo and Irri were written at the last minute because the rough-cut of the episode was running short, and filmed in post-production re-shoots. Note that all of these scenes are interior shots which didn't need to be filmed on-location.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup> </li> <li>It is never revealed in the show who sent the assassin to kill Bran and who is the real owner of the dagger. In the novel <i>"A Clash of Kings"</i> Catelyn is told to whom the dagger belonged; in the novel <i>"A Storm of Swords"</i> two characters figure out, based on circumstantial evidence, who sent the assassin. </li> <li>This is the first episode in which no deaths occurred. <ul><li>It is also the only episode in this season with no deaths. <ul><li>This makes it also the first episode without deaths before the start of the War of Five Kings. </li></ul> </li></ul> </li> </ul> <h2 id="Commentary" section="15">Commentary</h2> <p>Actors Isaac Hempstead Wright, Maisie Williams, and Sophie Turner provide an audio commentary for the episode on the DVD and Blu-ray releases. </p> <ul> <li> The young actors sing along with the main theme during the title sequence. </li> <li> Sean Bean was noted by the younger actors for secreting chocolate bars on his person and sneaking them to his young co-stars during filming. </li> <li> Conleth Hill apparently enjoyed trying to put Sophie Turner off during her close-ups. </li> <li> English-accented Sophie Turner and Irish-accented Jack Gleeson would imitate one another's accents for amusement between takes. </li> <li> Isaac Hempstead Wright's only scene with Jack Gleeson was a mock battle scene in the pilot, where Bran and Tommen have a mock fight while Joffrey and Robb watch on (this scene occurred in the book). This was not used in the final episode. </li> <li> The argument between Arya and Sansa at the breakfast table was Sophie Turner's first scene filmed for the first season. Maisie Williams dented the table with the knife so badly it had to be rotated several times to hide the marks from the camera. </li> <li> Mark Addy spooked one of the horses in the king's arrival scene in the first episode with his laugh, while he was talking to Isaac Hempstead Wright. </li> <li> Mark Addy's prosthetic beard was apparently easily removable so he could eat lunch without ruining it. </li> <li> Eugene Simon taught Maisie Williams and Sophie Turner some magic tricks between takes. </li> <li> Emilia Clarke was allowed to pick one of four horses for Daenerys's use in the series. </li> <li> Harry Lloyd was responsible for introducing the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/iPhone" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:iPhone">iPhone</a> game <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fruit_Ninja" class="extiw exitstitial" title="wikipedia:Fruit Ninja">Fruit Ninja</a></i> to the cast. It went on to become a craze on set, with cast and crew competing to see who could get the highest score. </li> <li> Joseph Mawle told Maisie Williams and Sophie Turner that if acting was easy, everyone would be doing it, so not to be worried if they found scenes hard because the producers had reasons for choosing them and they would be able to do the job. </li> <li> The producers considered asking Miltos Yerolemou to shave his hair off to match the appearance of Syrio Forel in the books, but decided he looked better with his hair. Author George R.R. Martin himself would later say in another Season 1 commentary that he didn't care about such minor differences, being more concerned with the satisfying performance of the actor. </li> </ul> <h2 id="In_the_books" section="16">In the books</h2> <dl><dd> <i>See: <a href="/wiki/Differences_between_books_and_TV_series_-_Season_1#Lord_Snow" title="Differences between books and TV series - Season 1">Differences between books and TV series - Season 1#Lord Snow</a> </i> </dd></dl> <ul><li> The episode is adapted from the following chapters of <i>A Game of Thrones</i>: <ul> <li> Chapter 18, Catelyn IV. </li> <li> Chapter 19, Jon III. </li> <li> Chapter 20, Eddard IV. </li> <li> Chapter 21, Tyrion III. </li> <li> Chapter 22, Arya II. </li> <li> Chapter 23, Daenerys III. </li> <li> Chapter 24, Bran IV. </li> </ul> </li></ul> <h2 id="Memorable_quotes" section="17">Memorable quotes</h2> <p><b><a href="/wiki/Jaime_Lannister" title="Jaime Lannister">Jaime Lannister</a></b>: "It must be strange for you, coming into this room.
