 <p></p> <p><b>"The Ghost of Harrenhal"</b> is the fifth episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_2" title="Season 2">second season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the fifteenth episode of the series overall. It premiered on April 29, 2012. It was written by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> and <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a> and directed by <a href="/wiki/David_Petrarca" title="David Petrarca">David Petrarca</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p>Confusion rages in the <a href="/wiki/Stormlands" title="Stormlands" class="mw-redirect">Stormlands</a> in the wake of a devastating reversal. <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn</a> must flee with a new ally, whilst <a href="/wiki/Littlefinger" title="Littlefinger" class="mw-redirect">Littlefinger</a> sees an opportunity in the chaos. <a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon</a> seeks to prove himself to his <a href="/wiki/Balon_Greyjoy" title="Balon Greyjoy">father</a> in battle. <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a> receives a promise from the enigmatic <a href="/wiki/Jaqen_H%27ghar" title="Jaqen H'ghar">Jaqen H'ghar</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> arrives at the <a href="/wiki/Fist_of_the_First_Men" title="Fist of the First Men">Fist of the First Men</a>. <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> receives a marriage proposal. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="In_the_Seven_Kingdoms" section="3">In the Seven Kingdoms</h3> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":3}' data-ref="3"><a href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/a1/RenlysDeathS2Ep5.png/revision/latest?cb=20160719015826"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20640%20358'%2F%3E" alt="RenlysDeathS2Ep5.png" width="640" height="358"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/a/a1/RenlysDeathS2Ep5.png/revision/latest?cb=20160719015826" alt="RenlysDeathS2Ep5.png" width="640" height="358"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>King Renly is assassinated.</figcaption></figure><p>King <a href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly Baratheon</a> is assassinated by the <a href="/wiki/Shadow" title="Shadow">Shadow</a> brought into the world by <a href="/wiki/Melisandre" title="Melisandre">Melisandre</a> and fathered by <a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a> just after agreeing to an alliance with King <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">Robb Stark</a>. His <a href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">kingsguard</a> <a href="/wiki/Brienne" title="Brienne" class="mw-redirect">Brienne</a> is mistakenly blamed for the death and she flees the camp with <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a>. Brienne later swears fealty to Catelyn. Renly's army disintegrates, with the forces of <a href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">House Tyrell</a> fleeing to <a href="/wiki/Highgarden" title="Highgarden">Highgarden</a> and the rest of his bannermen joining his brother King Stannis. Lord <a href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a> accompanies the Tyrell faction, sensing an opportunity to sway them to Joffrey's side. Ser <a href="/wiki/Davos_Seaworth" title="Davos Seaworth">Davos Seaworth</a> challenges King Stannis on his use of Melisandre's <a href="/wiki/Magic" title="Magic">magic</a> and convinces him to leave Melisandre behind when they attack <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> to quash rumors of her influence on him. Stannis places Davos in command of his fleet. When he is alone in his tent, Stannis briefly fights against the tears, showing he does in fact grieve for his younger brother. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Hand_of_the_King" title="Hand of the King">Hand of the King</a> <a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a> brings news of Renly's death to his sister, Queen Regent <a href="/wiki/Cersei_Lannister" title="Cersei Lannister">Cersei Lannister</a>. She is pleased, but Tyrion predicts Stannis's imminent attack. He tries to discuss plans for their defense, but she insists on keeping them secret. From his spy, his cousin Ser <a href="/wiki/Lancel_Lannister" title="Lancel Lannister">Lancel Lannister</a>, Tyrion learns that Cersei has been having extensive meetings with the <a href="/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild" title="Alchemists' Guild">Alchemists' Guild</a>.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":4}' data-ref="4"><a href="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/7/7a/Brienne_kills.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160802075758"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20768%20432'%2F%3E" alt="Brienne kills.jpg" width="768" height="432"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/7/7a/Brienne_kills.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160802075758" alt="Brienne kills.jpg" width="768" height="432"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Brienne kills a Kingsguard</figcaption></figure><p>He visits Wisdom <a href="/wiki/Hallyne" title="Hallyne">Hallyne</a> of the guild and learns that they have stockpiled thousands of jars of <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">Wildfire</a>, a dangerously flammable substance, to use as catapult ammunition. <a href="/wiki/Bronn" title="Bronn">Bronn</a> warns that this strategy could be disastrous, and result in panicking defenders accidentally burning down their own city. Tyrion orders Hallyne to answer to him instead of Cersei. Tyrion also passes a street protest that blames him for the ills of the city, as they believe Tyrion to be manipulating <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a>. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Theon_Greyjoy" title="Theon Greyjoy">Theon Greyjoy</a> takes command of his new ship and finds his crew insubordinate. His first mate <a href="/wiki/Dagmer_Cleftjaw" title="Dagmer Cleftjaw" class="mw-redirect">Dagmer Cleftjaw</a> tells him that he needs to prove himself to the men and Theon plans to raid <a href="/wiki/Torrhen%27s_Square" title="Torrhen's Square">Torrhen's Square</a> to draw the remaining forces of <a href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> from <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>. His plan is successful and Ser <a href="/wiki/Rodrik_Cassel" title="Rodrik Cassel">Rodrik Cassel</a> leads a force of 200 men to relieve his siege of Torrhen's Square. <a href="/wiki/Bran_Stark" title="Bran Stark">Bran Stark</a> provides well judged counsel to his subjects but continues to be troubled by his dreams.  </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":5}' data-ref="5"><a href="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/d/d0/Wildfire.png/revision/latest?cb=20160829160819"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201280%20720'%2F%3E" alt="Wildfire.png" width="1280" height="720"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/d/d0/Wildfire.png/revision/latest?cb=20160829160819" alt="Wildfire.png" width="1280" height="720"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Tyrion discovers the <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">Wildfire</a>.</figcaption></figure><p>He discusses dreaming of a <a href="/wiki/Three-eyed_raven" title="Three-eyed raven">three-eyed raven</a> with the wildling woman <a href="/wiki/Osha" title="Osha">Osha</a> but she declines to interpret the dream. He also confides dreaming of the sea flooding Winterfell and drowning his people, including Ser Rodrik. </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> hosts a protracted war council to determine a new strategy to deal with Robb. <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> serves as his <a href="/wiki/Cupbearer" title="Cupbearer">cupbearer</a>. Though he does not know who she really is, Tywin realizes that she is of northern extraction and questions her about her origins and opinions regarding Robb. She manages to avoid revealing her true identity. She leaves to fetch water and encounters <a href="/wiki/Jaqen_H%27ghar" title="Jaqen H'ghar">Jaqen H'ghar</a>, now a Lannister man-at-arms. Jaqen says that because she saved his life, and those of his two fellow prisoners, he owes her three deaths and offers to kill three people of her choosing. She targets <a href="/wiki/The_Tickler" title="The Tickler">the Tickler</a>, Ser <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a>'s torturer. </p> <p>The Tickler is soon found dead in the courtyard, his neck twisted 180 degrees. She is perturbed by the tricks of the warlock <a href="/wiki/Pyat_Pree" title="Pyat Pree">Pyat Pree</a>, who invites her to visit the <a href="/wiki/House_of_the_Undying" title="House of the Undying">House of the Undying</a>. </p> <p>The masked woman <a href="/wiki/Quaithe" title="Quaithe">Quaithe</a> delivers an enigmatic warning to Ser <a href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a> about Daenerys's need for protection from those who lust after her dragons. </ul> <h2 id="Notes" section="12">Notes</h2> <ul> <li>In the novel <i>A Clash of Kings</i> the series of murders at <a href="/wiki/Harrenhal" title="Harrenhal">Harrenhal</a> perpetrated by <a href="/wiki/Jaqen_H%27ghar" title="Jaqen H'ghar">Jaqen H'ghar</a> are attributed to a ghost. The episode features the murder of <a href="/wiki/The_Tickler" title="The Tickler">the Tickler</a> by Jaqen at <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a>'s request. </li> <li>This episode marks the death of King Renly Baratheon. <ul> <li>The night this episode aired, actor <a href="/wiki/Finn_Jones" title="Finn Jones">Finn Jones</a> (who plays Renly's lover, Ser Loras Tyrell) posted to his Twitter account, <i>"When the sun has set, no candle can replace it."</i><sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup> This is actually a famous quote that Loras uses later in the book series. </li> <li>Renly's death reduces the number of factions in the war to four: Joffrey Baratheon, Robb Stark, Balon Greyjoy, and Stannis Baratheon. Of course, due to geography if not ideology, Stannis isn't actively fighting Robb, because they're on opposite sides of the Lannisters' position in the middle of the continent. </li> <li>This only includes the factions that are currently fighting each other in open warfare. House Tyrell, House Martell, and House Arryn are still active players in the ongoing political machinations of Westeros, i.e. Margaery Tyrell in this episode. </li> </ul> </li> <li>For the first time since she left <a href="/wiki/Pentos" title="Pentos">Pentos</a>, Daenerys is brought up to speed on the current political situation in Westeros, including the death of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Baratheon" title="Robert Baratheon">Robert Baratheon</a>, and the fact that four rival kings now vie for the throne (word of Renly's death evidently not having reached across the Narrow Sea yet). </li> <li>Daenerys says "Dracarys" to <a href="/wiki/Drogon" title="Drogon">Drogon</a>, the young <a href="/wiki/Dragons" title="Dragons">dragon</a>, to encourage him to breathe fire. Dracarys (pronounced "drah-KAH-ris") is the <a href="/wiki/High_Valyrian" title="High Valyrian">High Valyrian</a> word for dragonfire. </li> <li>When Renly is meeting with Catelyn Stark, he says that the oath of fealty he wants Robb to swear will be the same one that Eddard Stark swore to Robert Baratheon "eighteen years ago". In Season 1, the events of <a href="/wiki/Robert%27s_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a> were consistently said to have occurred seventeen years ago. This reflects <a href="/wiki/Timeline#Differences_from_the_books" title="Timeline">the passage of time</a> by Season 2. </li> <li>Bronn refers to what Joffrey did to the "birthday present" that Tyrion sent him (which occurred in the <a href="/wiki/Garden_of_Bones" title="Garden of Bones">preceding episode</a>). In the culture of the <a href="/wiki/Seven_Kingdoms" title="Seven Kingdoms">Seven Kingdoms</a>, people actually use the term "<a href="/wiki/Nameday" title="Nameday">nameday</a>", not "birthday", because babies are named on the day they are born. "Nameday" has been consistently used on-screen throughout Season 1 and Season 2. This may have just been a dialogue error by the actor. </li> <li>Wisdom <a href="/wiki/Hallyne" title="Hallyne">Hallyne</a>, the head of the <a href="/wiki/Alchemists%27_Guild" title="Alchemists' Guild">Alchemists' Guild</a>, is played by <a href="/wiki/Roy_Dotrice" title="Roy Dotrice">Roy Dotrice</a>, who narrated the audiobook versions of all five novels currently in the <i>A Song of Ice and Fire</i> series (and thus in a sense is "reprising" his role as Hallyne). Roy Dotrice was originally supposed to play the role of Grand Maester Pycelle, but fell ill shortly before production on the TV series began and had to decline. His health later recovered, however, allowing him to join the cast as Hallyne. </li> </ul> <h2 id="In_the_books" section="13">In the books</h2> <dl><dd> <i>See: <a href="/wiki/Differences_between_books_and_TV_series_-_Season_2#The_Ghost_of_Harrenhal" title="Differences between books and TV series - Season 2">Differences between books and TV series - Season 2#The Ghost of Harrenhal</a> </i> </dd></dl> <ul><li> The episode is adapted from the following chapters of <i>A Clash of Kings</i>: <ul> <li> Chapter 20, Tyrion V: Tyrion inspects the Alchemists' Guild's wildfire with the pyromancer, Hallyne. </li> <li> Chapter 27, Daenerys II: <span>Daenerys stays at Xaro's palace and learns that King Robert is dead and that the Seven Kingdoms are at war.</span> </li> <li> Chapter 31, Arya VII: Jaqen H'ghar promises to kill three men of Arya's choosing in return for saving his life. </li> <li> Chapter 33, Catelyn IV: Renly is killed by a mysterious shade while Catelyn and Brienne are inside his tent. Catelyn and Brienne flee Renly's camp. </li> <li> Chapter 34, Jon IV: Lord Commander Mormont's expedition arrives at the Fist of the First Men. </li> <li> Chapter 35, Bran V: Bran continues to be troubled by his dreams. </li> <li> Chapter 36, Tyrion VIII: Cersei and Tyrion learn of Renly's death. </li> <li> Chapter 37, Theon III: Theon leads a force against Torrhen's Square. </li> <li> Chapter 39, Catelyn V: Catelyn and Brienne are heading back to Robb. </li> <li> Chapter 42, Davos II: Stannis and Davos plan to sail through the Blackwater attack King's Landing with ships. </li> <li> Chapter 43, Jon V: Qhorin Halfhand arrives at the Fist of the First Men.
