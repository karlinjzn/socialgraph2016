 <p></p> <p>"<b>Two Swords</b>" is the first episode of the <a href="/wiki/Season_4" title="Season 4">fourth season</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones" title="Game of Thrones" class="mw-redirect">Game of Thrones</a></i>. It is the thirty-first episode of the series overall. It premiered on April 6, 2014. It was written and directed by executive producers <a href="/wiki/David_Benioff" title="David Benioff">David Benioff</a> and <a href="/wiki/D.B._Weiss" title="D.B. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a>. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p><a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion</a> welcomes a <a href="/wiki/Oberyn_Martell" title="Oberyn Martell">guest</a> to <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a>. At <a href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> finds himself unwelcome. <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Dany</a> is pointed to <a href="/wiki/Meereen" title="Meereen">Meereen</a>, the mother of all <a href="/wiki/Slavery" title="Slavery">slave</a> cities. <a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya</a> runs into an <a href="/wiki/Polliver" title="Polliver">old friend</a>. </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="In_King.27s_Landing" section="3">In King's Landing</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Tywin_Lannister" title="Tywin Lannister">Tywin Lannister</a> has the ancestral <a href="/wiki/Valyrian_steel" title="Valyrian steel">Valyrian steel</a> greatsword of <a href="/wiki/House_Stark" title="House Stark">House Stark</a> , <a href="/wiki/Ice" title="Ice">Ice</a> (held by the Lannisters since <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">Eddard Stark</a>'s execution) melted down and re-forged into two smaller swords, after which he burns the wolf pelt scabbard that the greatsword was held in. He presents <a href="/wiki/Oathkeeper_(sword)" title="Oathkeeper (sword)">one</a> to his son <a href="/wiki/Jaime" title="Jaime" class="mw-redirect">Jaime</a> as a gift. Jaime is impressed, as the Lannisters have not owned a Valyrian steel sword of their own for generations. Tywin tells him he acquired the original sword from "someone who no longer needed it" and then commissioned a <a href="/wiki/Volantis" title="Volantis">Volantis</a> blacksmith to re-forge it. He wants Jaime to be his heir and return home to <a href="/wiki/Casterly_Rock" title="Casterly Rock">Casterly Rock</a>, but Jaime insists on remaining in the <a href="/wiki/Kingsguard" title="Kingsguard">Kingsguard</a>. Furious at Jaime's disobedience and lack of ambition, Tywin rebuffs him. He tells Jaime to keep the sword anyway.  </p> <p><a href="/wiki/Tyrion_Lannister" title="Tyrion Lannister">Tyrion Lannister</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bronn" title="Bronn">Bronn</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Podrick_Payne" title="Podrick Payne">Podrick Payne</a> wait on a road outside the city for the arrival of Prince <a href="/wiki/Doran_Martell" title="Doran Martell">Doran Martell</a> of <a href="/wiki/Dorne" title="Dorne">Dorne</a>, who has been invited to the <a href="/wiki/Purple_Wedding" title="Purple Wedding">royal wedding</a>. Tyrion quizzes Bronn on the sigils of the Dornish houses, none of which he knows, however Podrick answers perfectly. <a href="/wiki/Blackmont_(Lord)" title="Blackmont (Lord)">Lord Blackmont</a> approaches them on horseback and says Prince <a href="/wiki/Doran_Martell" title="Doran Martell">Doran</a> is in poor health and remained at <a href="/wiki/Sunspear" title="Sunspear">Sunspear</a>. Doran's brother, Prince <a href="/wiki/Oberyn_Martell" title="Oberyn Martell">Oberyn</a>, will be attending the wedding in his stead, and arrived before dawn. Assigning the reception of the Dornish Lords to the <a href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a>, Tyrion says they should find Oberyn as soon as possible "before he kills somebody or several somebodies". </p> <p>Prince Oberyn and his <a href="/wiki/Bastard" title="Bastard" class="mw-redirect">bastard</a> <a href="/wiki/Paramour" title="Paramour">paramour</a> <a href="/wiki/Ellaria_Sand" title="Ellaria Sand">Ellaria Sand</a> are examining prostitutes in a <a href="/wiki/Littlefinger%27s_brothel" title="Littlefinger's brothel">brothel</a> owned by <a href="/wiki/Petyr_Baelish" title="Petyr Baelish">Petyr Baelish</a>, but currently managed by <a href="/wiki/Olyvar" title="Olyvar">Olyvar</a>, as Lord Baelish is now in <a href="/wiki/Vale" title="Vale" class="mw-redirect">the Vale</a>. They choose the contortionist <a href="/wiki/Marei" title="Marei">Marei</a>, and Oberyn orders Olyvar to get undressed also. Oberyn is infuriated when he hears men singing <a href="/wiki/The_Rains_of_Castamere_(song)" title="The Rains of Castamere (song)">The Rains of Castamere</a>. He confronts the two Lannister soldiers, who insult him and Ellaria. Oberyn moves closer and chastises them for being "pink little men who are far too slow on the draw" rather than being "golden lions". One of the soldiers reaches for his sword, and Oberyn quickly stabs him through the wrist with a dagger and tells the other that his friend will only live if he immediately gets help. Tyrion and Bronn enter, and Oberyn and Tyrion step outside to speak privately. Oberyn says he wants revenge for the murder of his niece <a href="/wiki/Rhaenys_Targaryen" title="Rhaenys Targaryen" class="mw-redirect">Rhaenys</a>, his nephew <a href="/wiki/Aegon_Targaryen_(Son_of_Rhaegar)" title="Aegon Targaryen (Son of Rhaegar)" class="mw-redirect">Aegon</a>, and the rape and murder of his sister <a href="/wiki/Elia_Martell" title="Elia Martell">Elia</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Sack_of_King%27s_Landing" title="Sack of King's Landing">sack of King's Landing</a> by Lannister troops during <a href="/wiki/Robert%27s_Rebellion" title="Robert's Rebellion">Robert's Rebellion</a>. He wants <a href="/wiki/Gregor_Clegane" title="Gregor Clegane">Gregor Clegane</a>, who murdered Elia, and Tywin, whom Oberyn believes gave the order for Elia and her children to be killed. </p> <p>Tyrion, <a href="/wiki/Sansa_Stark" title="Sansa Stark">Sansa</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shae" title="Shae">Shae</a>, and another <a href="/wiki/Unidentified_handmaiden" title="Unidentified handmaiden">handmaiden</a> are sitting in a garden. Sansa is still distraught over the deaths of her <a href="/wiki/Robb_Stark" title="Robb Stark">brother</a> and <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">mother</a>. Tyrion attempts to console his wife but fails; Sansa has heard the gruesome details of the <a href="/wiki/Red_Wedding" title="Red Wedding">Red Wedding</a>, how the corpses of her mother and brother were desecrated. Sansa then leaves for the <a href="/wiki/Godswood" title="Godswood">godswood</a>, not to pray but because it is the only place no one talks to her. Tyrion returns and finds Shae in his bed. He scolds her because no one can find out about their relationship. She attempts to seduce him but he resists. She accuses him of trying to bribe her with diamonds and ship her away to the <a href="/wiki/Free_Cities" title="Free Cities">Free Cities</a>, but he doesn't know what she's talking about (<a href="/wiki/Varys" title="Varys">Varys</a> did this acting alone). She angrily leaves the room but is spotted by the handmaiden, who is hiding behind a pillar. </p> <p>Jaime, <a href="/wiki/Cersei" title="Cersei" class="mw-redirect">Cersei</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Qyburn" title="Qyburn">Qyburn</a> are in Cersei's chambers. Cersei has had a golden prosthetic hand made for Jaime, and Qyburn attaches it to Jaime's stump. Qyburn asks Cersei about her symptoms, and she says they are gone. After Qyburn departs, Jaime tells her that he will stay in the Kingsguard with one hand so he can stay close to her, then tries to kiss her, but she turns away, much to Jaime's confusion and anger. Cersei implies that she has taken other lovers and that their relationship has changed because of his lengthy absence, a statement that clearly wounds Jaime. The handmaiden suddenly enters and says she has important information for Cersei. </p> <p>As preparations for the royal wedding continue, <a href="/wiki/Margaery_Tyrell" title="Margaery Tyrell">Margaery Tyrell</a> and her grandmother <a href="/wiki/Olenna_Tyrell" title="Olenna Tyrell">Olenna</a> are interrupted by <a href="/wiki/Brienne_of_Tarth" title="Brienne of Tarth">Brienne of Tarth</a>, (Olenna being delighted to see the truth of the rumors about Brienne) who requests a private word with Margaery. Brienne tells Margaery the truth of <a href="/wiki/Renly_Baratheon" title="Renly Baratheon">Renly</a>'s death and vows to one day exact revenge on <a href="/wiki/Stannis_Baratheon" title="Stannis Baratheon">Stannis Baratheon</a>, whom she holds responsible. They walk past a statue of <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">King Joffrey</a> standing over a dead direwolf, commemorating the victory over the northern rebellion. </p> <p>Elsewhere in the <a href="/wiki/Red_Keep" title="Red Keep">Red Keep</a>, Jaime, Ser <a href="/wiki/Meryn_Trant" title="Meryn Trant">Meryn Trant</a> and King <a href="/wiki/Joffrey_Baratheon" title="Joffrey Baratheon">Joffrey</a> discuss security at the upcoming wedding. Jaime is clearly irked by Joffrey's arrogance as well as Meryn's sycophantic agreement with the king's bragging that he is responsible for keeping the people fed (Jaime notes that this is more the doing of the <a href="/wiki/House_Tyrell" title="House Tyrell">Tyrells</a>) and claiming he singlehandedly won the war. Jaime (who had previously noted to his father that thousands in <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> alone still want Joffrey dead) points out that the war is far from won; Stannis is still alive and will probably raise fresh armies. Dismissive of the threat posed by Stannis following the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>, Joffrey leafs through the <a href="/wiki/Book_of_Brothers" title="Book of Brothers" class="mw-redirect">Book of Brothers</a>, which details the history of the Kingsguard and those who served in it. He reads Jaime's brief entry and sneers that someone must have forgotten to record all of his uncle's great deeds. He also questions Jaime's ability to serve in the Kingsguard with one hand. Jaime defensively quips that using his left hand makes for more of a challenge. </p> <p>As Sansa prays alone in the godswood, she is watched by Jaime and Brienne; Brienne expects Jaime to uphold his oath to return <a href="/wiki/Catelyn_Stark" title="Catelyn Stark">Catelyn Stark</a>'s daughters in return for his freedom, but Jaime counters by pointing out the situation is complicated by Catelyn's death and Sansa's marriage to Tyrion. As Sansa is leaving the godswood, she is approached by <a href="/wiki/Dontos_Hollard" title="Dontos Hollard">Dontos Hollard</a>, who thanks her for convincing Joffrey to <a href="/wiki/The_North_Remembers" title="The North Remembers">spare his life</a> and gives her what he claims to be his mother's necklace as a token of gratitude. </p> <h3 id="In_the_North" section="4">In the North</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Ygritte" title="Ygritte">Ygritte</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tormund" title="Tormund">Tormund</a> stand outside a stone ravine. Tormund chides her for failing to finish off <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a>. Suddenly, their scouts signal that someone is approaching. <a href="/wiki/Styr" title="Styr">Styr</a> and a band of <a href="/wiki/Thenn" title="Thenn" class="mw-redirect">Thenn</a> approach them through the ravine. Out of earshot, Tormund comments about how much he hates Thenns. Styr says his party raided a village south of the ravine and gathered meat. He says the meat tastes so much better south of the <a href="/wiki/Wall" title="Wall" class="mw-redirect">Wall</a>, and chides Tormund for losing their <a href="/wiki/Orell" title="Orell">warg</a> as well as Jon. He reminds Tormund how the <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> are feasting and getting fat at <a href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a>. Styr suggests that Tormund taste crow meat before he dies, as a human arm roasts over a fire. </p> <h3 id="At_the_Wall" section="5">At the Wall</h3> <p>At <a href="/wiki/Castle_Black" title="Castle Black">Castle Black</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> has survived the <a href="/wiki/Mhysa" title="Mhysa">injuries</a> inflicted by Ygritte and has largely recovered, though he still grimaces while getting dressed and walks with a slight limp. Having been told of Robb's death, Jon shares with <a href="/wiki/Samwell_Tarly" title="Samwell Tarly">Samwell Tarly</a> that he had always been jealous of Robb for having more of their father's affection and for being better than him at riding and fighting, and with girls. Despite all this, he says he could never bring himself to hate Robb. Sam responds by saying he has felt the same way about Jon, that Jon is better than him at everything (except reading).  </p> <p>It turns out that Sam had been sent to retrieve Jon, who has been summoned to testify before a panel of five sworn brothers, including Maester <a href="/wiki/Aemon" title="Aemon">Aemon</a>, acting Lord Commander <a href="/wiki/Alliser_Thorne" title="Alliser Thorne">Alliser Thorne</a>, First Builder <a href="/wiki/Othell_Yarwyck" title="Othell Yarwyck">Othell Yarwyck</a>, and the exiled former <a href="/wiki/Lord_Commander" title="Lord Commander">Lord Commander</a> of the King's Landing <a href="/wiki/City_Watch" title="City Watch">City Watch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Janos_Slynt" title="Janos Slynt">Janos Slynt</a>. Both Thorne and Slynt are openly hostile to Jon, disbelieving much of what he says. At the hearing, Jon admits to killing <a href="/wiki/Qhorin_Halfhand" title="Qhorin Halfhand" class="mw-redirect">Qhorin Halfhand</a> but says that it was not murder, rather Qhorin wanted Jon to kill him to give Jon credibility with the <a href="/wiki/Wildling" title="Wildling" class="mw-redirect">wildlings</a> in order to get a spy into Mance's army. Slynt dismisses Jon as "the bastard son of a <a href="/wiki/Eddard_Stark" title="Eddard Stark">traitor</a>" while Thorne, a friend of Qhorin's, reacts with incredulity that Jon would presume to act like he knew the Halfhand. Jon responds by saying that Thorne should know that Qhorin would have done anything to protect the <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a>. </p> <p>When Jon refers to the wildlings as "Free Folk," Slynt accuses him of going native, to the extent he even talks like them. Jon snaps back, acknowledging that he talks like a wildling and also admits to living amongst the wildlings, eating with them, and climbing the Wall with them. He catches himself before admitting that he lay with one, but says it anyway. Slynt calls for Jon's execution for breaking one of his vows. Aemon points out the impracticality of executing every ranger who lays with a girl and says that "the Wall would be manned by headless men." Thorne says that there is a difference between sneaking off to a brothel and sleeping with the enemy. </p> <p>Jon does not defend himself at this point. Instead, he says that <a href="/wiki/Mance_Rayder" title="Mance Rayder">Mance Rayder</a> intends to attack the Night's Watch with his army of 100,000 wildlings and gives intelligence on Mance's plan of attack. Both Thorne and Slynt disbelieve what Jon has to say. Aemon, however, realizes that Jon is speaking the truth, and the hearing ends with Jon being allowed to keep his head, although after Jon leaves, Thorne expresses his distrust for Jon. </p> <h3 id="In_the_Riverlands" section="6">In the Riverlands</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Arya_Stark" title="Arya Stark">Arya Stark</a> and the "Hound" <a href="/wiki/Sandor_Clegane" title="Sandor Clegane">Sandor Clegane</a> are still traveling through the <a href="/wiki/Riverlands" title="Riverlands" class="mw-redirect">Riverlands</a> on a single horse. They pass by overturned carts and dead bodies, including those of children. Arya asks when she is going to get her own horse, complaining of the <a href="/wiki/Hound" title="Hound" class="mw-redirect">Hound</a>'s stench. He scoffs at the idea, noting that horses are hard to come by and that he wouldn't give Arya her own horse (even if one was available) as she is the only thing of value he has since he did not take anything of value when he <a href="/wiki/Blackwater" title="Blackwater">left</a> King's Landing. Arya assures him she has no plans to run off, saying that she would die by nightfall without him as she has no one to go to. The Hound reminds her that she still has an aunt in the <a href="/wiki/Vale" title="Vale" class="mw-redirect">Vale</a>, <a href="/wiki/Lysa_Arryn" title="Lysa Arryn">Lysa Arryn</a>, to whom he plans to ransom Arya. </p> <p>The two eventually come upon a tavern. Both hungry, Arya suggests that they attack the men there, to which the Hound expresses reluctance as there are five men. Just then, two men exit to relieve themselves. Arya recognizes one of them as <a href="/wiki/Polliver" title="Polliver">Polliver</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Lannister" title="Lannister" class="mw-redirect">Lannister</a> man-at-arms who had <a href="/wiki/What_is_Dead_May_Never_Die" title="What is Dead May Never Die">previously</a> taken Arya's sword <a href="/wiki/Needle" title="Needle">Needle</a> and killed her friend <a href="/wiki/Lommy" title="Lommy">Lommy</a>. She also notices that Polliver still has Needle and runs off to get it back. The Hound chases after her, stopping her at the tavern's door, but they are seen and choose to go in anyways. </p> <p>It is clear that Polliver and the other men are making trouble and intend to rape the tavern owner's daughter. Arya and the Hound go to a table far away from everyone else, but Polliver still recognizes the Hound and walks over to their table and strikes up a conversation. He does not recognize Arya, who he thinks is just the Hound's latest sexual conquest. Polliver implies that he and his men intend to rape and pillage on their way back to King's Landing and invites the Hound to come along. Polliver says that he and his men wear the King's colors and can do whatever they like, to which the Hound leans forward and states emphatically, "Fuck the King." </p> <p>The conversation then becomes tense and turns into a standoff. They eventually draw their swords. At first, the Hound fights the five men alone while Arya stands in the corner. He easily dispatches two of them and fights off the rest, stabbing one in the groin. Another, however, gets him on the ground, holding a dagger to his throat. The Hound is able to fight him off, stabbing him repeatedly in the head with his own dagger. </p> <p>Arya then joins the fray, smashing a pot over the head of one of the men and stabbing him with his own long sword. She then slashes Polliver's leg from behind, causing him to fall to the ground. Arya takes Needle from Polliver and stands over his prone form. She echoes what he had said to Lommy just before he killed him, though Polliver still doesn't recognize her and has no idea what she is talking about. Arya then repeats what he had said to her when he took Needle: "Fine little blade. Maybe I'll pick my teeth with it." It is at that point when Polliver recognizes her and becomes frightened. Arya then kills Polliver in the same way he had killed Lommy by driving Needle slowly into his throat. Arya and the Hound then leave the tavern, taking with them a horse for Arya and a chicken the Hound wanted. </p> <h3 id="Across_the_Narrow_Sea" section="7">Across the Narrow Sea</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a> sits near the sea with her three dragons, each one about the size of a small horse. Daenerys strokes <a href="/wiki/Drogon" title="Drogon">Drogon</a>'s head, while <a href="/wiki/Rhaegal" title="Rhaegal">Rhaegal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Viserion" title="Viserion">Viserion</a> fight over a dead lamb, Drogon joins the fight. As Daenerys tries to calm him down, without warning, Drogon snaps at her with a hiss, a warning to not interfere. He then roars and goes to contest the kill. </p> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":3}' data-ref="3"><a href="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/f/fe/Drogon_snarls_at_his_mom.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20140407194747"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%201166%20718'%2F%3E" alt="Drogon snarls at his mom.jpg" width="1166" height="718"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette4.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/f/fe/Drogon_snarls_at_his_mom.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20140407194747" alt="Drogon snarls at his mom.jpg" width="1166" height="718"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Drogon snarls a warning at his mother.</figcaption></figure><p>This act, that her own children would threaten her, and that they are outgrowing her influence and ability to rein them, leaves Daenerys visibly shaken. <a href="/wiki/Jorah_Mormont" title="Jorah Mormont">Jorah Mormont</a> approaches and tells her that dragons can never be tamed, not even by their mother. </p> <p>Daenerys returns to her <a href="/wiki/Unsullied" title="Unsullied">Unsullied</a> army to resume the march to <a href="/wiki/Meereen" title="Meereen">Meereen</a>. She notices <a href="/wiki/Grey_Worm" title="Grey Worm">Grey Worm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Daario_Naharis" title="Daario Naharis">Daario Naharis</a> are absent and sets out to find them after being told they are "gambling". She finds the two men sitting face-to-face holding their swords in front of them; they have been contesting their strength since midnight. Daario explains they are deciding on which of them will ride up front with her in the vanguard. Frustrated, Daenerys states that the honor goes to <a href="/wiki/Ser" title="Ser" class="mw-redirect">Ser</a> <a href="/wiki/Jorah" title="Jorah" class="mw-redirect">Jorah</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barristan_Selmy" title="Barristan Selmy">Ser Barristan</a> as they did not keep her waiting all morning. She orders the two men to ride at the back with the livestock. Daenerys also adds that the last man holding his sword shall find a new queen to fight for and both men immediately drop their weapons. </p> <p>On the road to Meereen, Daenerys speaks with <a href="/wiki/Missandei" title="Missandei">Missandei</a>, who tells her queen that Meereen would be wise to fear her approach. Daario meets them on the cliff side, and Daenerys expresses her annoyance when he shows her flowers he has picked. He reveals that the flowers represent a portrait of the landscape, and serve various purposes, chiefly that knowing her surroundings is important to Daenerys' strategy. </p> <p>The marching army halts, and Daenerys goes to the forefront, discovering a slave child crucified upon a cross. The child is dead, her hand pointing the way to Meereen, and Ser Jorah tells Daenerys there is one for each mile to the last of the great slave cities, 163 in total. Ser Barristan offers to have outriders go ahead to bury them, but Daenerys refuses, ordering that each of them be buried, and their collars removed, but not before she has looked upon each and every Ice has symbolized the Stark family as it has been a heirloom to them for many generations. When Tywin melted down the Valyrian steel sword, he thought it ended the Stark line and legacy. When Arya later retrieves her own sword Needle from Polliver, she finds another Stark sword which rebirths the Stark ability to act in the world through her.<sup id="cite_ref-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-0">[1]</a></sup> </li></ul> <figure class="article-media-thumbnail" data-component="article-media-thumbnail" data-attrs='{"ref":4}' data-ref="4"><a href="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/81/Theon_Greyjoy.png/revision/latest?cb=20110302142633"> <img class="article-media-placeholder" src="data:image/svg+xml;charset=utf-8,%3Csvg%20xmlns%3D'http%3A%2F%2Fwww.w3.org%2F2000%2Fsvg'%20viewBox%3D'0%200%20864%20486'%2F%3E" alt="Theon Greyjoy.png" width="864" height="486"><noscript> <img src="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/gameofthrones/images/8/81/Theon_Greyjoy.png/revision/latest?cb=20110302142633" alt="Theon Greyjoy.png" width="864" height="486"> </noscript> </a> <figcaption>Ice's wolf-pelt scabbard, seen in the first episode of Season 1.</figcaption></figure><ul> <li>The wolf pelt scabbard that Tywin burns in the opening scene was used to carry Eddard's greatsword Ice during all of its appearances in Season 1. Weiss">D.B. Weiss</a>, after Season 3's "<a href="/wiki/Walk_of_Punishment" title="Walk of Punishment">Walk of Punishment</a>". While both of them co-directed these episodes, due to the way that Director's Guid of America rules work, only one of them could be <i>officially</i> credited with directing each one. Benioff was given the formal credit for "Walk of Punishment", so now it was Weiss's turn to be the only one formally credited as director for "Two Swords".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[2]</a></sup> </li> <li> <a href="/wiki/Elia_Martell" title="Elia Martell">Elia Martell</a>, wife of <a href="/wiki/Rhaegar_Targaryen" title="Rhaegar Targaryen">Rhaegar Targaryen</a>, has not been mentioned by name in previous seasons (though her story is detailed in the "Histories &amp; Lore" animated Blu-ray featurettes). After Rhaegar was killed and Tywin's army sacked King's Landing, his soldiers killed Rhaegar's young daughter and infant son (Daenerys's niece and nephew as well as Oberyn's), and Ser Gregor Clegane personally raped and killed Princess Elia. Ser Gregor was never punished. In the books, what happened to Elia and her children was fully explained in the first novel. In the aftermath of the Red Wedding (corresponding to the Small Council scene in the Season 3 finale), Tywin is surprised that the Freys killed Catelyn Stark, because he wanted her taken alive. Tyrion then brings up that Elia Martell was also supposed to be taken alive when Tywin's army sacked King's Landing. Outside of the animated featurettes, the TV series itself has only mentioned it in passing on a few occasions: in Season 1's "<a href="/wiki/Baelor" title="Baelor">Baelor</a>" when Maester Aemon explains that he was a Targaryen and his outrage that the Lannisters killed his family, "even the little children", and then in Season 3's "<a href="/wiki/Kissed_by_Fire" title="Kissed by Fire">Kissed by Fire</a>" when Thoros of Myr remarks that he saw the butchered royal children in the aftermath of the sacking of the city. </li> <li>The Dornish embassy that Tyrion meets when they arrive at the city are carrying the banners of six different Dornish Houses. In the left column, from front to back: <a href="/wiki/House_Dalt" title="House Dalt">House Dalt</a>, <a href="/wiki/House_Manwoody" title="House Manwoody">House Manwoody</a>, and <a href="/wiki/House_Jordayne" title="House Jordayne">House Jordayne</a>. In the right column, from front to back: <a href="/wiki/House_Blackmont" title="House Blackmont">House Blackmont</a>, <a href="/wiki/House_Qorgyle" title="House Qorgyle">House Qorgyle</a>, and <a href="/wiki/House_Gargalen" title="House Gargalen">House Gargalen</a>. Podrick identifies Dalt, Manwoody, and Blackmont in dialogue, the other three are known from their sigils in the books. In the novels, the embassy carried the banners of <i>nine</i> Dornish Houses: apart from these six, there was also the banner of <a href="/wiki/House_Martell" title="House Martell">House Martell</a>, of <a href="/wiki/House_Uller" title="House Uller">House Uller</a>, and House Allyrion. In the book version Oberyn was with the embassy, but in the TV version he rode ahead of the other envoys, explaining why the Martell banner is not present with the others. His paramour Ellaria Sand is a bastard daughter of House Uller (presumably why their banner was also not present). It is unclear why the Allyrions were not present. </li> <li> <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Cogman" title="Bryan Cogman">Bryan Cogman</a> explained that the change from having Oberyn arrive with the embassy in the novels to riding ahead so he is first seen in a brothel wasn't just to build up the tension of his introduction, but self-consciously meant to parallel how Tyrion himself was first introduced in the TV series - "rogue member of the family doesn't show up as expected, finds his way into a brothel": when Tyrion first appeared in "<a href="/wiki/Winter_is_Coming" title="Winter is Coming">Winter is Coming</a>", he was similarly expected in the royal embassy that arrived at Winterfell, but instead he rode ahead, characters asked where he was, and then the camera first shows him in a brothel. As they leave the brothel, Tyrion even overtly draws the comparison that both he and Oberyn are second sons and considered the black sheep of their respective families.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[3]</a></sup> </li> <li>Cogman also explained that originally, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Book_of_Brothers" title="The Book of Brothers">The Book of Brothers</a></i> recording the famous deeds of all the great Kingsguard was actually meant to feature predominantly in the episode he wrote, "<a href="/wiki/Oathkeeper" title="Oathkeeper">Oathkeeper</a>", as Jaime was looking through it and talking to Brienne (similar to how it is introduced in the novels, in which Jaime is reading through it and talking to another Kingsguard). Jaime is still seen leafing through it in a few subsequent scenes this season, but the major focus on it was shifted to this episode later in production. As Cogman explained, the writers came to the realization that Joffrey doesn't have any major scenes before his wedding in the next episode, so they wanted to invent something for him to do now in the intervening episode; thus the scene of Jaime discussing the book (apparently with Brienne) was shifted to Joffrey thumbing through the book and remarking on it to Jaime.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[4]</a></sup> </li> <li>Cogman also said that he was tasked with plotting out the overview for Arya's story arc across Season 4, and in this episode specifically he originally intended for Arya and the Hound's fight to just be against another Frey patrol they stumbled into, ending with Arya stealing one of their horses (as she as tired of riding on the same horse as Sandor). The fight with Polliver was going to happen later in the season. Benioff and Weiss, however, "very wisely" felt that it would make a much stronger season opener if the confrontation happened in this episode, with Arya dramatically killing Polliver, avenging Lommy, and getting her sword Needle back.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[5]</a></sup> </li> <li>Bronn remarks, "I need a <a href="/wiki/Heraldry" title="Heraldry">sigil</a>" when Podrick is describing the different sigils carried by the Dornish envoys. This is a nod to the fact that in the books, Bronn actually has obtained a sigil of his own by this point in the story. Bronn played a vital role in the <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Blackwater" title="Battle of the Blackwater">Battle of the Blackwater</a>, pulling a large chain across the entrance of the bay to trap Stannis's fleet inside once the <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">wildfire</a> trap was sprung. Following his knighting, Bronn was permitted to claim his own knightly sigil: he chose a flaming green chain on a smoking grey field (green because wildfire burns with green flames) to commemorate the battle in which he earned his knighthood. The TV series condensed the battle so the chain subplot was removed, but it did still have Bronn play a vital role in the wildfire trap, by having him personally fire a flaming arrow which ignited the leaking fire ship filled with thousands of jars of wildfire. As the chain was omitted from the TV series, Bronn's sigil based on that omitted subplot was also removed. </li> <li>Despite appearing in the opening credits, <a href="/wiki/Dragonstone_(castle)" title="Dragonstone (castle)">Dragonstone</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Dreadfort" title="The Dreadfort">the Dreadfort</a>, <a href="/wiki/Winterfell" title="Winterfell">Winterfell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Meereen" title="Meereen">Meereen</a> do not appear in the episode; Meereen's associated storyline (Daenerys') is the only one to be featured. </li> <li>When Arya takes back Needle, she repeats what Polliver had said to her previously when he took Needle from her in "<a href="/wiki/What_is_Dead_May_Never_Die" title="What is Dead May Never Die">What is Dead May Never Die</a>".
