 <p></p> <p><b>Sons of Winter</b> is the fourth of six episodes of <i><a href="/wiki/Game_of_Thrones:_A_Telltale_Games_Series" title="Game of Thrones: A Telltale Games Series">Game of Thrones: A Telltale Games Series</a></i>. It was released on May 26, 2015 for PC/Mac, PlayStation 4 and PlayStation 3, on May 27th for Xbox One and Xbox 360, and on May 28th for iOS and Android. </p> <p><br></p> <h2 id="Plot" section="1">Plot</h2> <p><i>The fate of the scattered Forresters hangs in the balance, and no one allied with the family is safe. An unexpected meeting offers Rodrik a new opportunity to free Ironrath from its Whitehill stranglehold, but with young Ryon Forrester still held prisoner, his next moves are critical. Gared must abandon Castle Black and venture beyond the Wall in search of the mysterious North Grove, risking encounters with Wildlings and worse. In King's Landing, the population is reeling from a recent royal death, and Mira must step carefully in an increasingly complex game of politics where secrets are traded as currency. Far away in Essos, Asher infiltrates the slave city of Meereen at the behest of Daenerys Targaryen, but will the friendship he holds dear break under the strain of a mission that raises memories best forgotten?</i> </p> <h2 id="Summary" section="2">Summary</h2> <h3 id="At_the_Wall" section="3">At the Wall</h3> <p>The <a href="/wiki/Night%27s_Watch" title="Night's Watch">Night's Watch</a> burns <a href="/wiki/Britt_Warrick" title="Britt Warrick">Britt's</a> corpse, and <a href="/wiki/Gared_Tuttle" title="Gared Tuttle">Gared Tuttle</a> is charged with murdering a brother. Finn can possibly testify on Gared's behalf. Regardless, <a href="/wiki/Denner_Frostfinger" title="Denner Frostfinger" class="mw-redirect">Frostfinger</a> decides to have Gared imprisoned. <a href="/wiki/Jon_Snow" title="Jon Snow">Jon Snow</a> will pull Gared aside, but no matter what is said, Jon and his men will leave for <a href="/wiki/Craster%27s_Keep" title="Craster's Keep">Craster's Keep</a> without him. Gared is then thrown into a small storeroom as a makeshift prison cell, until Frostfinger can execute him. </p> <p>The guard watching his cell leaves to grab some food, which gives Gared the opportunity to look for a way out. He finds a trowel behind a bag of food, takes it, and then uses it on a loose stone at the bottom hinge of the door. <a href="/wiki/Cotter" title="Cotter">Cotter</a> will show up to help break through the rock, and the two make their escape. After avoiding being spotted by a guard, <a href="/wiki/Finn" title="Finn">Finn</a> will show up and ask to join Gared and Cotter in their escape north of <a href="/wiki/The_Wall" title="The Wall">the Wall</a>. Cotter does not want Finn to go with them, but the decision is ultimately up to Gared. If Gared chooses to bring Finn, he will join Gared and Cotter. Gared can still allow Finn to join after initially choosing to leave Finn behind, but he'll angrily stay behind if Gared tells Finn that his mind is made up. </p> <h3 id="Across_the_Narrow_Sea" section="4">Across the Narrow Sea</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Asher_Forrester" title="Asher Forrester">Asher Forrester</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beskha" title="Beskha">Beskha</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Malcolm_Branfield" title="Malcolm Branfield">Malcolm Branfield</a> are brought before <a href="/wiki/Daenerys_Targaryen" title="Daenerys Targaryen">Daenerys Targaryen</a>. Daenerys warns the group that, as their Mother, she doesn't tolerate lies about her dragons. <a href="/wiki/Viserion" title="Viserion">Viserion</a> flies into the tent, and Daenerys dares Asher to try and touch him. If Asher tries to pet Viserion, Daenerys will appreciate Asher's bravery, but it won't affect the outcome. When Daenerys asks for proof of Asher's encounter with <a href="/wiki/Drogon" title="Drogon">Drogon</a>, Asher will give her the dragon tooth. Daenerys will become defensive and concerned about Drogon attacking people. She denies Asher's request to borrow some <a href="/wiki/Mercenary" title="Mercenary">sellswords</a>. When it is revealed that Beskha has been to <a href="/wiki/Meereen" title="Meereen">Meereen</a> before, Daenerys insists that they must help the fight in Meereen if they want her help. Regardless of whether Asher or his uncle volunteers Beskha, she will be incredibly angry at having to take part in the mission. </p> <p>Asher later finds Beskha drinking and in an even worse state. <a href="/wiki/Croft" title="Croft">Croft</a> scolds Beskha for her behavior, and they begin to fight. Asher can break up the fight or take a side, but the outcome is the same regardless and Beskha then attacks Asher. If Asher chooses not to fight back, he will still be unharmed. If he chooses to fight, Beskha will be relatively undamaged. Regardless, Beskha will finally admit why Meereen has been bugging her all this time: Beskha used to be a slave to one of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Masters" title="Great Masters">Great Masters</a> of Meereen. She was kidnapped as a toddler, and sold into slavery. She was eventually thrown into the fighting pits and forced to fight other slave children. While she talks, Asher can get her another drink. Otherwise, she'll get one herself. She tells Asher that she knows Meereen well, but she also fears it. </p> <p>Asher, Beskha, and the rest of Daenerys's men prepare for the attack on Meereen. While the slaves are being armed and prepare for the uprising, Asher and his group will seize the eastern beacon before they have the chance to warn the Great Masters of the coming attack and slave rebellion. Asher is ordered to follow Croft's every direction, or he will not receive any reward. Led by Beskha, Asher and Croft sneak around through Meereen's alleys to avoid the guards' gaze. As Asher scouts the area, a slave is discovered and brought out into the street to be beaten. After the guards leave, Beskha goes to the slave's side to help his passing. Enraged at the slave's death, Beskha runs off down another alley. Asher and Croft follow her and find her pulling a Great Master out of his house and beating him to the ground. She reveals him to be her former slave master: Dezhor zo Raza. She is intent on taking her revenge, but this goes against the orders of both Daenerys and Croft. Asher either kills <a href="/wiki/Dezhor_zo_Raza" title="Dezhor zo Raza">Dezhor zo Raza</a> himself, stops Croft and allows Beskha to kill her former master, or stops Beskha from killing Dezhor zo Raza. Regardless of Asher's decision, the group leaves to take care of the beacon. </p> <p>Asher, Beskha, and Croft reach the beacon and need to carefully take out each guard they encounter. After slaughtering a number of guards, a few spot the group and try to light the beacon. Asher manages to kill the one last guard trying to light the beacon, the attack is successful, and the way is prepared for the coming revolt. </p> <h3 id="At_Ironrath" section="5">At Ironrath</h3> <p>With <a href="/wiki/Gryff_Whitehill" title="Gryff Whitehill">Gryff Whitehill</a> as the new "<a href="/wiki/Lord_of_Ironrath" title="Lord of Ironrath">Lord of Ironrath</a>", Lord <a href="/wiki/Rodrik_Forrester" title="Rodrik Forrester">Rodrik Forrester</a> is forced to train in secret, with Ser <a href="/wiki/Royland_Degore" title="Royland Degore">Royland Degore</a> in the Ironwood Grove. At first, Rodrik doesn't do well, but Royland teaches Rodrik a trick: hitting an opponent behind the knees, and helping Rodrik win a fight despite his injured leg. After the training, <a href="/wiki/Elaena_Glenmore" title="Elaena Glenmore">Elaena Glenmore</a> will show up with her younger brother, <a href="/wiki/Arthur_Glenmore" title="Arthur Glenmore">Arthur</a>. Regardless of whether Elaena agreed to wed Rodrik, Elaena reveals that Lord <a href="/wiki/Ludd_Whitehill" title="Ludd Whitehill">Ludd Whitehill</a> has strong-armed <a href="/wiki/Glenmore_(Lord)" title="Glenmore (Lord)">her father</a> into forcing her to marry Gryff instead. Elaena is displeased with this situation and refuses to marry Gryff. She requests that Rodrik help her get rid of Gryff. If Rodrik agrees, Arthur will loan <a href="/wiki/House_Glenmore" title="House Glenmore">House Glenmore's</a> elite guard to House Forrester. </p> <p>As Rodrik and Elaena prepare for their attack on the <a href="/wiki/House_Whitehill" title="House Whitehill">Whitehills</a>, they hear that Gryff has attacked Maester <a href="/wiki/Ortengryn" title="Ortengryn">Ortengryn</a>. The guard who <a href="/wiki/Talia_Forrester" title="Talia Forrester">Talia Forrester</a> bit has had his wound become infected, and Gryff accuses the maester of deliberately allowing the wound to fester. The Glenmore soldiers prepare to ambush the Great Hall as Rodrik and Elaena enter to find Gryff beating the maester. Gryff will be surprised to see Elaena and threatens her. Rodrik toys with Gryff and then summons the Glenmore men when he's ready. The Glenmore soldiers surround Gryff and his men. Rodrik can show Gryff mercy by not hitting him, or only hitting Gryff once or twice and stopping before he takes any serious injury. Rodrik can also beat Gryff to a pulp. He can't actually kill Gryff, but Rodrik can maim Gryff's left eye and knock Gryff out. Regardless of what Rodrik does with Gryff, Lady <a href="/wiki/Elissa_Forrester" title="Elissa Forrester">Elissa Forrester</a> insists that word must not reach Lord Whitehill and demands that all of <a href="/wiki/Ironrath" title="Ironrath">Ironrath's</a> ravens be killed. </p> <p>With the Glenmore men to secure the keep, Ironrath is finally back under the control of <a href="/wiki/House_Forrester" title="House Forrester">House Forrester</a>. Talia and Lady Forrester will comment on what Rodrik did to Gryff. Then, a message is received from <a href="/wiki/Highpoint" title="Highpoint">Highpoint</a>: Lord Whitehill has extended an invitation to discuss a truce. With Gryff now in Forrester custody, House Forrester can bargain for <a href="/wiki/Ryon_Forrester" title="Ryon Forrester">Ryon Forrester</a>. Rodrik will be walking into dangerous territory, but he could have some extra protection if the Glenmores came with him. Ser Royland will advise bringing the Glenmore soldiers to Highpoint as protection, while <a href="/wiki/Duncan_Tuttle" title="Duncan Tuttle">Duncan Tuttle</a> will advise leaving the Glenmore soldiers to guard Ironrath. After Rodrik has decided what to do with the Glenmore soldiers, he needs to choose whether to bring Duncan or Ser Royland with him to Highpoint. Rodrik's final decision before he leaves is to give Talia a task. </p> <p>When Rodrik and his group return to Ironrath, everyone is missing (including the Glenmore troops, if Rodrik chose to leave them at Ironrath). If the Glenmore troops were brought along to Highpoint, two of them will walk alongside Rodrik as he enters Ironrath. If they were left Rodrik will find an arrow and drops of blood. After hearing a laugh inside the hall, Rodrik enters to find <a href="/wiki/Ramsay_Bolton" title="Ramsay Bolton">Ramsay Snow</a> talking and eating with Talia. </p> <h3 id="At_King.27s_Landing" section="6">At King's Landing</h3> <p>Whispers fill the hallways of <a href="/wiki/King%27s_Landing" title="King's Landing">King's Landing</a> on the day of <a href="/wiki/Tommen_Baratheon" title="Tommen Baratheon">Tommen's</a> coronation, and <a href="/wiki/Mira_Forrester" title="Mira Forrester">Mira Forrester</a> listens closely to what is being said. Mira meets with the coal boy, <a href="/wiki/Tom" title="Tom">Tom</a>. Tom confirms that Lord Ludd Whitehill is sending lots of gold to someone in King's Landing. Mira has two suspects: the ironwood merchants, Lord <a href="/wiki/Rickard_Morgryn" title="Rickard Morgryn">Morgryn</a> and Lord <a href="/wiki/Andros" title="Andros">Andros</a>. She decides to investigate by sneaking into King Tommen's coronation feast. </p> <p>Despite being uninvited, Mira shows up to the feast. She finds <a href="/wiki/Sera_Durwell" title="Sera Durwell" class="mw-redirect">Sera "Durwell"</a>, who agrees to help her sneak into the party. Lord <a href="/wiki/Garibald_Tarwick" title="Garibald Tarwick">Garibald Tarwick</a> will approach the ladies and is formally introduced to Mira. He has a short conversation with them. Lord Tarwick and Sera will then leave Mira, freeing her to explore the feast. Mira can eavesdrop on various party-goers. After eavesdropping on Lord Andros, Mira can talk with Lord Rickard Morgryn to reveal that Lord Andros has pushed Lord Morgryn out of the ironwood business and Lord Whitehill now works exclusively with Lord Andros. <a href="/wiki/Lyman_Lannister" title="Lyman Lannister">Lyman Lannister</a>, a distant third cousin to the main line of [, has taken Lord Morgryn's place and fulfills Morgryn's role in Lord Andros's ironwood deal. Mira tries to talk with Lyman, but Lord Andros yells at him to stop talking with her. </p> <p>Lady <a href="/wiki/Margaery_Tyrell" title="Margaery Tyrell">Margaery Tyrell</a> will show up to the feast, but thankfully Sera hides Mira from her line of sight. While Sera attends to Lady Margaery, Lord Tarwick will check on Mira and warn her about Lyman. When Mira needs answers about why Lyman is dangerous, Lord Tarwick asks Mira for the truth about the identity of Sera's family, because the last Durwells supposedly died out over two centuries ago. If Mira vouches for Sera's family and lies that House Durwell survives as a small, unnoticed family, then Lord Tarwick will decide to propose to Sera. If Mira reveals the truth that Sera is a bastard, Lord Tarwick will end his courtship of Sera. Regardless of how Mira answers his question, Lord Tarwick will reveal that Lyman suffered a head wound at a tournament and has been addicted to milk of the poppy ever since. Mira uses this information to coax information out of Lyman, and he reveals that Lord Andros is using the gold from Lord Whitehill to hire hundreds of sellswords to destroy the Forresters. </p> <h3 id="North_of_the_Wall" section="7">North of the Wall</h3> <p>Gared, Cotter, and Finn (if Gared chose to bring him along) argue as they wander <a href="/wiki/Beyond_the_Wall" title="Beyond the Wall">Beyond the Wall</a>. They see a <a href="/wiki/Wildling" title="Wildling" class="mw-redirect">wildling</a> and Cotter decides to go and talk with her, hoping that she'll trust him. However, since Cotter is still wearing his Night's Watch cloak, the wildling doesn't trust the "Crow". Gared and his group will end up being surrounded by wildlings. Regardless of whether Gared chooses to stay hidden or attack, they will have to fight the wildlings. Cotter will be injured, but he assures Gared that they can rest up ahead. </p> <p>Gared and his group arrive at a small wildling camp. Cotter says it's abandoned and asks Gared to fetch him something to drink. Gared goes into a tent in the back to investigate, but a youngwildling girl comes up behind Gared and threatens to kill him. As Gared tries to talk her down, Cotter comes to investigate. Cotter recognizes her as his little sister <a href="/wiki/Sylvi" title="Sylvi">Sylvi</a> and hugs her. </p> <h3 id="At_Highpoint" section="8">At Highpoint</h3> <p><a href="/wiki/Gwyn_Whitehill" title="Gwyn Whitehill">Gwyn Whitehill</a> greets Lord Rodrik Forrester and his company and welcomes them to Highpoint. Gwyn will question Rodrik's intentions as they walk to the dining hall. If the Glenmore soldiers were brought, they will follow Rodrik through Highpoint. She'll mention that she kept her promise of pushing for peace, citing the invitation as proof. They stop at a tapestry depicting the Whitehill family. When you get to the hall, Gwyn will go off to get her father, leaving Rodrik, his mother, his advisor, and possibly the Glenmore soldiers locked inside with Whitehill crossbowmen aiming at them from above. Rodrik can talk with his group as he searches for an exit. </p> <p>After Rodrik has looked around the room, Gwyn will return with Lord Ludd Whitehill and the meeting will begin. Lord Whitehill begins with small talk, and makes a toast. After the toast, he finally makes his proposal: House Whitehill will continue to harvest the ironwood, but will allow House Forrester to work under the Whitehills to make more weapons for <a href="/wiki/House_Bolton" title="House Bolton">House Bolton</a> and share in the profit (at a much smaller percentage). Rodrik and Lady Forrester refuse, so Lord Whitehill threatens to order Gryff Whitehill to burn Ironrath to the ground. </p> <p>Rodrik then reveals that Gryff has been imprisoned, and offers to trade Gryff for Ryon Forrester. Lord Whitehill responds by bringing in Ryon and threatening to kill him unless Rodrik gives in. Even if Rodrik threatens to kill Gryff, Lord Whitehill won't budge and claims not to care about his fourthborn son. Rodrik can accept Lord Whitehill's ironwood deal, on the condition that Lord Whitehill follow through on a trade of Gryff for Ryon. Rodrik can also call Lord Whitehill's bluff, that he isn't willing to risk sacrificing Gryff. If Rodrik begs for mercy, Lord Whitehill will spare Ryon. Regardless of Rodrik's choice, Lord Whitehill is willing to trade Gryff for Ryon. </p> <p class="MsoNormal"> </p> <h2 id="In-Game_Decisions" section="9">In-Game Decisions</h2> <p>This list shows some of the choices made by players that are tracked by the game. </p> <p><b>Who brought Finn along to the north?</b> </p> <ul> <li>Brought Finn -
